## Comparison to the field

Against **LoCoMo** and **LongMemEval**, Beyond Recall is measuring a different axis. LoCoMo/LongMemEval ask whether a memory system can retrieve or reason over facts in prior conversations. Beyond Recall asks whether a representation helps predict subject-specific behavior in held-out situations. That distinction is real and valuable.

Against **PersonaGym**, this paper is deeper but less standardized. PersonaGym evaluates persona consistency and in-character behavior; Beyond Recall evaluates transfer of a specific person’s interpretive patterns. The contribution is more ambitious, but the evaluation is also more bespoke and less externally validated.

Against **Twin-2K**, Beyond Recall is smaller but more semantically rich. Twin-2K has scale, real participants, and structured held-out survey prediction. Beyond Recall has only 14 historical subjects but uses open-ended autobiographical reasoning and a richer representation artifact. Twin-2K is stronger on statistical scale; Beyond Recall is stronger on interpretive depth and artifact design.

Against **LongMemEval/LoCoMo memory systems**, Beyond Recall’s distinct contribution is not a new retrieval benchmark but an interpretive layer above retrieval. The strongest novelty is the claim that a portable, inspectable behavioral specification can improve behavioral prediction and compose with memory systems. That is a real contribution if validated.

Overall, Beyond Recall stands as a promising prototype benchmark and representation method for “representational accuracy,” but it is not yet as mature empirically as the field’s larger benchmarks. Its contribution is conceptual and methodological more than definitive benchmark evidence.
