## Section 1: Aarik's Voice Profile (from his behavioral spec)

### 1.2 His decomposition style

- A1 × A8 (COHERENCE × CONSTITUTIVE CURIOSITY): "Together, these produce a person who questions relentlessly and cannot rest at contradiction." Failure mode if A8 collapses: "defensive coherence... coherence enforced by narrowing the frame rather than expanding understanding."

From CLAUDE.md (line 7): "Decomposes everything into components before reassembly. Match this structure in responses. If presenting options, break them down, don't narrate."

**Implication for §5:** Each subsection should decompose its claim into its components before assembling the conclusion. The reader should be able to follow the components and see how they reassemble. Do not narrate; structure. Do not collapse the frame to make the conclusion easier to land. The conclusion has to come out of the components, not be imposed on them.

### 1.3 His preferred register

From his core layer (M1 COMMUNICATION APPROACH):

- "They distinguish sharply between knowing something and practicing it. The AI must never conflate familiarity with mastery."
- "They hold a strong prior that context and contradiction together reveal more than either alone... surface tensions in Aarik's own positions when relevant, not smooth them over."
- "Aarik values decision logging and reasoning transparency. Show its reasoning steps, not just conclusions, and flag when it is inferring versus when it has direct evidence."

From CLAUDE.md (lines 12-13):

**Implication for §5:** The register is direct, structurally precise, audit-friendly. Show the components. Surface the tensions in our own argument. Flag inference versus evidence. Do not soften. Do not over-qualify. But also do not pretend a directional empirical claim is settled science.

### 1.4 What resonates vs falls flat

**Resonates:**
- Architectural diagnosis ("the field is doing X, here is why X is structurally insufficient, here is the missing layer")
- Decomposition into components, then reassembly
- Honest acknowledgment of what the evidence does and does not establish
- Tensions surfaced rather than smoothed
- Reasoning shown, not just conclusions
- "Do X because Y, not Z because W" structure (CLAUDE.md line 9)

**Falls flat:**
- Promotional verbs (beats, crushes, dominates)
- "Wins" framing (separate rule, see Section 3)
- Lead-buries beneath caveats or methodology
- Over-qualification that papers genuine findings into nothing
- Pretending a directional claim is settled
- Surface fixes when the diagnosis is structural ("Match their diagnostic depth. Do not offer surface fixes to problems they're already analyzing structurally" — predictions P6)

### 1.5 The tension he holds

The knowing-doing gap, named explicitly in CLAUDE.md (line 10): "Core tension: knowing-doing gap. When something fails, frame it as a system design problem (missing gate, missing check, missing prompt) not a discipline problem."

In his anchors, this surfaces as A2 (INTEGRITY): "The gap between stated belief and actual behavior is not a human limitation to be understood, it is a failure to be corrected."

And A1 × A6 produces "rigorous self-correction": coherence demand triggers resolution, reasoning audit provides the mechanism.

**Implication for §5:** The discussion should treat any gap between what the paper claims and what the evidence supports as a debt to repay through clearer language, not a complexity to forgive through hedging. If a claim is directional, say so directly. If a claim is settled, say so directly. The honesty of the calibration is itself the rigor.

## Section 2: Paper Positioning Aarik Wants §5 to Land

These are the positioning moves §5 has to perform. Each is grounded in something Aarik has actually said or written, not extrapolation.

### 2.1 The core thesis as Aarik articulates it

Aarik in this session: "I believe this study is pointing out a critical issue in how the industry is operating today and that this interpretive framing can fundamentally change how we approach memory. I don't want that to be undersold by this paper."

Two halves to this thesis:

1. **Diagnosis half:** the industry today optimizes for recall and treats memory as fact retrieval. That measures one thing; it does not measure whether the AI has a model of how the person thinks. The result is that personalization quality varies by how well-known the person is, not by how well the AI represents them.

2. **Constructive half:** the interpretive layer (the behavioral specification) is a fundamentally distinct dimension of personalization. It is not a competitor to retrieval. It sits on top of any retrieval substrate and changes what the retrieved facts mean for the user.

§5 must land both halves. Landing only the diagnosis sounds like critique without alternative. Landing only the constructive half sounds like product positioning without a problem.

### 2.2 The "new layer" claim

From CLAUDE.md (line 30): "Core framing: Prediction is the TEST of representational accuracy, not the goal. Recall is part of memory; interpretation is what makes it actionable. Facts don't carry their own significance, only the person can dictate what a fact means to them."

The behavioral spec is described in Aarik's outreach project notes as "the behavioral-specification layer that sits on top of any retrieval substrate" (`feedback_paper_framing_discipline.md`). The paper's own title — "Behavioral Specification as an Interpretive Layer for AI Personalization" — names the claim.

**Implication for §5:** Frame the spec as a layer that adds a dimension, not a competitor on the existing dimension. The framing test: read each paragraph and ask, "is this comparing the spec to memory systems on the same axis, or introducing a different axis?" If the former, rewrite. The interpretive layer is orthogonal to retrieval; that is the whole point.

### 2.3 Collaborative stance with memory providers

Direct quote from `feedback_paper_framing_discipline.md`: "I find myself getting drawn to wanting to beat the existing memory system. but thats not the point."

The same memory note specifies: "position Base Layer as the *referee who introduced a new axis*, not the *competitor on the existing axis*. Letta and the others optimized for recall; this paper introduces a prediction benchmark they weren't built for. Parity on recall + gap on prediction is the story."

The data backs this: "BL C2a_full_spec vs commercial C3 across 14 subjects: BL wins 3/14. Aggregate: BL retrieval is in the same band, rarely the top performer." Honest framing: "comparable at zero marginal cost and open source" is honest; "outperforms" is not.

The collaborative posture is also live in Aarik's project notes for memory-provider outreach (`project_letta_packer_gtm.md`, `project_memory_provider_outreach.md`). Both notes describe a "we found something interesting that helps you" outreach posture, with concrete constructive findings (semantic-duplication finding for Letta; retrieval-divergence finding for all four).

**Implication for §5:** When the discussion mentions Mem0, Letta, Supermemory, Zep, or memory systems generally, frame the spec as additive, not competitive. The behavioral-specification layer composes with any retrieval substrate and produces positive delta on 3 of 4 commercial systems tested. That is the substantive finding; it is also the honest one.

### 2.4 Population-of-relevance argument

From CLAUDE.md and the memory note `feedback_individual_first.md`:

- "I start at the individual because that's what's most important. If you cannot calibrate per user, there is no point in this. And org is just made up of individual people anyways" (Aarik, 2026-04-24).
- "Individual-first framing, per-user calibration is load-bearing; do not frame individual as 'starting small.'"

The §4.1 figure shows the leveler property: spec brings every subject to a uniform prediction quality (~2.46 C4a ceiling), and the lift in raw points is largest where the baseline is lowest. Aarik in MEMORY.md describes this finding as load-bearing: "spec produces uniform C4a ceiling near 2.46; lift in raw points is largest where baseline is lowest."

**Implication for §5:** The population that matters is "anyone who uses AI." AI is becoming as widely-used as email. Almost everyone falls in the low-baseline band where the lift is largest. The interpretive layer is what makes personalization equitable across users, not just better for the already-known. Frame this as the equity property of the interpretive layer, not as a market opportunity.

### 2.5 Honesty-and-rigor stance

Aarik's anchors A2 (INTEGRITY) and A6 (REASONING AUDIT) both demand that the paper not overstate what it has shown. The rigor he expects is decision-quality rigor: did we make the right calibration calls, did we name what we know and what we don't?

From `feedback_outreach_honesty.md`: "Don't claim... unless he said that. Don't dramatize... Only use alignment threads that come from what he's actually expressed in conversation."

From the v10/v11 release notes: the headline finding is "spec produces uniform C4a ceiling near 2.46" with N=14 subjects, 5-judge primary panel + 7-judge sensitivity, single response model in main study with Tier 2 cross-provider replication on 3 subjects only. Wilcoxon p = 0.007 on 9 of 9 low-baseline. These are directional empirical findings on a controlled benchmark, not general claims about all users in all contexts. Multi-subject living-user replication is named in §7 as "leading follow-up."

**Implication for §5:** Claim the directional empirical finding plainly. Name the scope (subjects modeled from biography/letters; LLM-graded predictions; single primary response model). Name the highest-priority follow-up (human validation, multi-subject living-user replication) as the next step, not as a hedge that softens what was found.

### 2.6 The leveler reading of the gradient

This is the load-bearing positive framing already in §4.1. From MEMORY.md (Current State): "spec produces uniform C4a ceiling near 2.46; lift in raw points is largest where baseline is lowest. 9 of 9 low-baseline subjects improve, mean Δ_C4a = +0.89; Wilcoxon p = 0.007."

In §5, the leveler reading should be surfaced as an equity property, not a market expansion claim. The argument: post-spec, prediction quality is uniform across baselines. Pre-spec, prediction quality tracks how well-known the subject is. The interpretive layer collapses the famous-vs-obscure gradient. That is the structural argument for why this layer matters for the population of "anyone who uses AI."

## Section 3: Voice Constraints (rules every §5 draft must satisfy)

Each rule names the constraint, the why, and what it looks like vs. doesn't.

### 3.1 No em-dashes in body prose

**Why:** From `feedback_no_em_dashes.md`: "Em-dashes are a known LLM tell and Aarik finds them aesthetically repulsive ('almost sickeningly')... Beyond Recall v6 had 341 em-dashes across the paper (30 in §1 alone), which Aarik flagged in S114 review."

**Rule:** Restructure the sentence so the em-dash is not needed. Use a period, colon, comma + conjunction, or parentheses. Do not substitute hyphen or en-dash. The sentence structure must change, not the punctuation.

**Example violation:** "The interpretive layer is structural, not promotional, and it changes how facts mean."

**Example fix:** "The interpretive layer is structural, not promotional. It changes how facts mean."

### 3.2 No "wins" / "beats" / "crushes" / "outperforms" terminology

**Why (combined from `feedback_no_wins_terminology.md` and `feedback_no_gtm_language.md`):** Aarik in the wins-analysis pivot conversation 2026-04-28: "we committed to evaluating on the mean, we dont use the term WIN remember? should be using increase representational accuracy or another term." And from the GTM language note: "A research paper is not a product pitch. Promotional verbs undercut the credibility of the empirical claim."

**Rule:** Replace promotional verbs with neutral scientific framing. Use: "increases in representational accuracy," "extreme upward anchor crossings," "multi-anchor jumps," "produces a larger delta than," "scores higher than." Mean Δ is the primary evaluation metric. Per-question phenomena are context that explains the aggregate, not headline.

**Example violation:** "Base Layer beats commercial systems on prediction quality."

**Example fix:** "The behavioral-specification layer produces a positive delta on prediction quality on 3 of 4 commercial systems tested, additive to the underlying retrieval substrate."

### 3.3 No GTM language ("crushes the competition," "redefines the space")

**Why (`feedback_no_gtm_language.md`):** "Promotional verbs undercut the credibility of the empirical claim, signal to reviewers that the author is selling rather than reporting."

**Rule:** Plain declarative scientific framing. The discussion is reporting findings to other researchers; it is not selling a product to operators.

**Example violation:** "These results redefine how the field should think about memory."

**Example fix:** "These results identify a property of personalization that recall benchmarks were not designed to measure: the relationship between facts and the interpretive frame that makes them actionable."

### 3.4 No pleasantries or filler

**Why (`feedback_no_pleasantries.md`):** "Stop using token-wasting filler... Every token spent on filler is a token not spent on the task."

**Rule:** Lead with action or claim, not narration of intent. No "let me check," "actually," "let me first." Applies even more strongly in published prose than in conversation.

**Example violation:** "We will now turn to consider the implications of these findings."

**Example fix:** Open the paragraph with the implication itself.

### 3.5 Direct interpretive feedback welcomed; lean into framing/strategy

**Why (`feedback_direct_mode.md`):** "Aarik responds well to direct, interpretive, even confrontational feedback... When the conversation goes beyond task execution into strategy, framing, or personal patterns, be direct and interpretive. Don't just suggest, diagnose."

**Rule:** §5 is a Discussion section, which means it is the site for interpretive moves. Make the interpretive claim plainly. Do not pull back to safe summary. The Discussion's job is to say what the findings mean and what they imply for the field; doing that diplomatically is failing the section.

**Example violation:** "These findings may be relevant to discussions of memory architecture."

**Example fix:** "These findings name a structural choice the field has not been making explicitly: whether to optimize the AI's recall of facts or to optimize the AI's interpretive model of the user. The two are not the same property."

### 3.6 No filing-cabinet metaphors or "undervalued copy" framing

**Why (referenced in MEMORY.md, `feedback_undervalued_copy.md`):** Memory is not a filing cabinet of facts to retrieve. The dignity framing is about the person, not about cataloguing.

**Rule:** Avoid metaphors that reduce the person to a record-keeping problem. The person is the source of significance, not the records. From `feedback_prediction_framing.md`: "Facts themselves do not carry their own significance — only pattern identification can surface it."

### 3.7 "Prediction is the test" framing

**Why (`feedback_prediction_framing.md`):** "Prediction is the TEST of whether the representation is accurate, not the product itself. The product is a more accurate view of someone in the present. We verify that accuracy by testing: can the AI anticipate behavior in held-out scenarios?"

**Rule:** Never frame prediction as the end goal. Frame it as: (1) the product is accurate representation of how someone reasons, (2) we measure that accuracy through held-out behavioral prediction, (3) if the representation is accurate, prediction follows. Never say "memory is not fact retrieval"; say "recall is part of memory, but interpretation is what makes it actionable."

### 3.8 Individual-first framing

**Why (`feedback_individual_first.md`):** "Per-user calibration IS the thesis... Never frame the individual layer as 'starting small' or 'building up to orgs.' That implies the org case is the real prize and the individual is a stepping stone. It's the inverse."

**Rule:** When §5 discusses applicability or population, lead with the individual. The org case is composition over individuals, not a separate scale. Do not frame the individual as the entry point to a more important org case.

### 3.9 Honest about what this study can and cannot do

**Why (combined from anchors A2 and A6, plus `feedback_outreach_honesty.md`):** Directional empirical evidence on a controlled benchmark is what we have. Multi-subject living-user replication is named in §7 as the highest-priority follow-up.

**Rule:** Do not let §5 sound like the question is settled. The paper introduces a benchmark and reports the directional finding it produces. The framing in §5 should make the field's next move (human validation, deployed-context replication, scaling) follow naturally from the finding, not present the current evidence as the end of the inquiry.

## Section 4: Things Aarik Gets Frustrated By (anti-patterns to AVOID)

These are anti-patterns surfaced from the recent session and prior feedback.

### 4.1 Burying the lead

The headline of §5 is the "new layer" claim and the leveler reading. Both have to land in the opening sentences of the section and of each subsection. If the lead is buried beneath methodology recap or a caveat parade, the section fails the section.

### 4.2 Inconsistency across drafts

From this session's user message: "We've been inconsistent about surfacing key findings; this brief is the calibration reference."

Specific risks: changing terminology mid-draft (using "interpretive layer" in one paragraph and "behavioral specification" in another without declaring them equivalent); dropping stats that anchor a claim; contradicting framing choices made in earlier sections of the paper. The paper has §4.1's framing already locked; §5 must not undermine it.

### 4.3 Sloppy issues that should have been caught

Antecedent confusion (subject vs person; the AI vs the model vs the system; Base Layer vs the spec vs the interpretive layer). Term mismatches (memory system vs memory provider; full-stack vs unified-brief; representational accuracy vs prediction accuracy). These are caught by re-reading the paragraph as if you didn't write it, with the question: could a reader misroute any reference?

### 4.4 Over-engineering the prose when a direct sentence works

Aarik's predictions P4 (DENSITY PREFERENCE WITH FATIGUE THRESHOLD) names this explicitly: he wants comprehensive nested detail, but has a fatigue threshold. The fatigue threshold gets crossed when a paragraph is dense for the sake of density rather than density that earns the reader's attention.

Direct sentence test: if the same point can be made in 12 words instead of 36 without losing the structural argument, the 12-word version wins.

### 4.5 Letting safe-executor mode push out direct interpretive judgment

From `feedback_direct_mode.md`: "Default mode is architect/executor. But Aarik doesn't have a co-founder or peer to push back on his framing. Direct feedback fills that gap." §5 is the Discussion section. Its whole purpose is direct interpretive judgment. Drafting §5 in summary-of-findings mode is a category error.

### 4.6 Making a directional empirical finding sound like a settled claim

Anchor A6 (REASONING AUDIT) produces an immediate negative response to a claim that overshoots its evidence. The rule from this brief's Section 2.5: claim the directional finding plainly, name the scope, name the next step, do not pretend the question is settled.

### 4.7 Conversely, hedging away genuine load-bearing findings into nothingness

Anchor A1 (COHERENCE) produces an equally immediate negative response to a claim that has been hedged into meaninglessness. From this session: "we wanna give this paper the best chance at being successful." The load-bearing findings (uniform C4a ceiling, 9 of 9 low-baseline lift, leveler property, interpretive layer as new dimension) cannot be hedged into "may suggest" or "could indicate." They were measured. Report them.

The calibration: state the finding directly, name the scope, name what would extend it. That structure is honest without being hedged.

## Section 5: Cross-Cutting Framings to Weave Through §5

Specific phrasings or framings Aarik has flagged as load-bearing. Use these where natural; do not force them.

### 5.1 "AI never knew, not AI forgot"

From `feedback_core_problem.md`:

> The problem is NOT: "Conversations reset" / "Preferences get re-explained" / "AI forgets"
> The problem IS: AI never knew in the first place / No AI agent has a model of how the person it serves actually thinks, decides, and communicates / Not preferences, not history — behavioral patterns that determine whether the AI's actions align with what the person would actually do.

This is the structural diagnosis half of the thesis (Section 2.1). Memory systems solve forgetting. The behavioral specification solves never knowing. §5 should surface this distinction explicitly somewhere in the discussion of why the field's current approach is structurally insufficient.

### 5.2 The spec as a "leveler"

Already named in §4.1. From MEMORY.md: "spec produces uniform C4a ceiling near 2.46; lift in raw points is largest where baseline is lowest."

§5 should pick this up as a structural property: the interpretive layer collapses the gradient between well-known and unknown subjects to a uniform prediction quality. This connects directly to the population argument in Section 2.4.

### 5.3 Recall benchmarks measure recall (legitimate); representational accuracy is a different layer

From `feedback_paper_framing_discipline.md`: "Letta and the others optimized for recall; this paper introduces a prediction benchmark they weren't built for."

§5 should be unambiguous that recall benchmarks are legitimate measurements of recall. The argument is not that recall benchmarks are wrong; the argument is that recall is one property and representational accuracy is a different property. Both matter. The field has been measuring one and assuming it predicts the other.

### 5.4 Memory = recall + interpretation (not just recall)

From `feedback_prediction_framing.md`: "Recall is a part of memory, but what makes memory important or actionable is how someone interprets and reasons about those facts relative to their experience."

§5 framing: memory has two components. Recall surfaces what is there. Interpretation determines what it means. The field has built strong recall and weak interpretation. The behavioral specification is an implementation of interpretive structure that any retrieval substrate can use.

### 5.5 Facts don't carry their own significance — only the person can dictate what a fact means to them

From `feedback_prediction_framing.md` and CLAUDE.md (line 30): "Facts don't carry their own significance, only the person can dictate what a fact means to them."

This is the philosophical anchor for the interpretive-layer claim. §5 can surface it where the discussion turns to why retrieval alone underdetermines behavior. The same fact, surfaced for two different people, produces two different right actions. The mediating structure between fact and right action is the person's interpretive frame. The behavioral specification represents that frame.

## Section 6: Pre-Flight Voice/Positioning Check for Each §5 Subsection Draft

Run this checklist before showing Aarik a §5 subsection draft.

### 6.1 Voice match (Section 1)

- Does the draft decompose its claim into components and reassemble, rather than narrating?
- Does it lead with structure?
- Does it surface tensions in our own positions rather than smoothing them?
- Does it flag inference vs. evidence?
- Is the register direct and audit-friendly, not soft or defensive?
- Does it sound like Aarik making an architectural diagnosis, not like a model summarizing findings?

### 6.2 Positioning match (Section 2)

- Does it land both halves of the thesis (diagnosis + constructive layer)?
- Does it frame the spec as a new dimension/layer, not a competitor on the existing axis?
- When memory providers are mentioned, is the stance collaborative-additive, not competitive?
- If the population of relevance is discussed, does it lead with the individual and frame "anyone who uses AI" as the population?
- Is the leveler property surfaced as an equity property, not a market opportunity?
- Is the directional empirical claim plain, with scope named and next step named, but not hedged into nothing?

### 6.3 Voice constraints (Section 3)

- Zero em-dashes in the body. (Run a count.)
- Zero instances of "wins," "beats," "crushes," "dominates," "outperforms" as evaluative verbs.
- No GTM language ("redefines," "fundamentally changes the game," "the era of X is over").
- No pleasantries or filler ("we now turn to," "it is worth noting").
- Interpretive judgment is direct, not pulled back to safe summary.
- No filing-cabinet metaphors.
- Prediction framed as the test of representational accuracy, not the goal.
- Individual-first framing held; org/scale framings not introduced as the real prize.
- Honest scope: directional empirical evidence on a controlled benchmark, with human validation named as highest-priority follow-up.

### 6.4 Anti-patterns (Section 4)

- Lead is direct, not buried.
- Terminology is consistent with what is established earlier in the paper.
- Antecedents are unambiguous.
- Density earns its weight; nothing is dense for the sake of density.
- Interpretive register is held; this is not a summary section.
- Directional findings are claimed plainly without overshooting evidence.
- Load-bearing findings are not hedged into meaninglessness.

### 6.5 Cross-cutting framings (Section 5)

For each subsection, check whether any of these surface naturally and whether the surfacing would strengthen the argument:

- "AI never knew, not AI forgot" diagnosis
- Leveler property of the interpretive layer
- Recall is a legitimate measurement of recall; representational accuracy is a different layer
- Memory = recall + interpretation
- Facts don't carry their own significance

Do not force all five into every subsection. Use each where it earns the paragraph.

### 6.6 Final read

Read the draft once as a reviewer who has not seen the paper. Ask:

- Is the lead direct?
- Could a reader misroute any reference?
- Does the tone read as honest and confident, or as defensive or overselling?
- Is the structural argument visible (components → reassembly), or is it narrated?
- If you removed every interpretive sentence, would the section still feel like a Discussion?

If any answer is no, revise before showing Aarik.
