## Method

- **Conditions:** `C1_supermemory_fp` (Supermemory retrieval only) vs `C3_supermemory_fp` (Supermemory retrieval + spec).
- **5-judge primary panel:** Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.6, GPT-4o, GPT-5.4.
- **7-judge sensitivity:** adds Gemini Flash and (where available) Gemini Pro.
- Per (subject, condition, judge): mean across 39 questions.
- Per (subject, condition): mean across judges in panel.
- Aggregate: mean across subjects (subject = unit of inference).
- Δ_spec = C3 − C1 per subject, then mean across subjects.

**Paid-tier rerun subjects:** `bernal_diaz`, `babur`, `cellini`, `rousseau` — judged 2026-04-23 at the canonical source path. The other 9 global subjects use existing canonical judgments. Hamerton's `supermemory_fullpipeline` data lives only at the study-repo mirror (`memory-study-repo/results/hamerton/`); it is included here to match prior coverage (n = 14).

## Per-question mixture (5-judge primary)

Nominal design: 14 subjects × 39 questions × 2 conditions = 546 paired (C1, C3) questions across all subjects.

Parse-failure caveat: on C1_supermemory_fp, the 5-judge panel emitted parse-failure scores (score=0, parse_failure=True) for some question-judge cells. Following study convention (`recompute_5judge_primary.py`, `compute_memory_systems_5judge.py`), parse-failure rows are excluded from per-question and subject aggregates. After that exclusion, pairs where neither C1 nor C3 has any valid judge score on the 5-judge panel drop out. Effective paired count is smaller than the nominal 546.
