### The single discrepancy: line 1711

**Current text (§6 Sample constraints):**
> The main-study response model is Haiku 4.5 across all 14 subjects; an additional **4** response models appear in the §4.6.1 Tier 2 cross-provider replication on 3 subjects only.

**Problem:** "4" is wrong. The Tier 2 set is exactly **2** response models (Sonnet 4.6 and Gemini 2.5 Pro), as established explicitly in §3.5 (line 402), §4.6.1 (line 1428), §5 (line 1532), Appendix C.3 (lines 2241-2242), and the Appendix H glossary (line 3286).

**Likely cause:** The "4" appears to be a v9-era or v10-draft-era artifact carried over from earlier wording when the paper was contemplating 4 additional Tier 2 response models (consistent with the CLAUDE.md "v10 changes from v9" note: "Six-model coverage scoping HONESTY: main study Haiku-only across 14 subjects; 4 additional response models appear in Tier 2 cross-provider replication on 3 subjects only"). The downstream tightening to 2 models was applied in §3.5, §4.6.1, §5, Appendix C, and the glossary — but missed in the §6 limitations paragraph.

**Proposed corrected line:**
> The main-study response model is Haiku 4.5 across all 14 subjects; an additional **2** response models (Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 2.5 Pro) appear in the §4.6.1 Tier 2 cross-provider replication on 3 subjects only.

This wording matches the §5 summary (line 1532) almost verbatim and brings §6 into alignment with §3.5, §4.6.1, and Appendix C.

## 5. Downstream propagation: 5-of-6 math check

Multiple lines in the paper claim "5 of 6 cells reproduce" the spec direction in Tier 2. The cell count math:

- 3 subjects (Ebers, Yung Wing, Zitkala-Sa)
- × 2 response models (Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 2.5 Pro)
- = **6 (subject × response-model) cells**

This matches:
- Line 114: "5 of 6 (subject × response-model) cells" ✓
- Line 1428: "3 subjects × 2 response models = 6 (subject, response model) cells" ✓
- Line 1432: "5 of 6 cells" ✓
- Line 1446: "3 subjects × 2 response models (one Anthropic, one Google) ... five of the six cells" ✓
- Line 1709: "five of six non-Haiku cells" ✓
- README: "The paper reports 5 of 6 cells positive in this replication" ✓

The 5-of-6 math is internally consistent and consistent with the data footprint.

If line 1711's "4 response models" were taken literally, the cell count would be 3 × 4 = 12 cells (or 3 × (1+4) = 15 cells if Haiku is counted as a baseline), which would contradict every "5 of 6" statement elsewhere in the paper. This is further evidence that line 1711 is the outlier and the rest of the paper is consistent.
