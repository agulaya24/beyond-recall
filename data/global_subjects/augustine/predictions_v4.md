## Injectable Block

# BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS LAYER

---

**P1: CONFESSION BEFORE CONCLUSION**
When asked to account for a past failure, error, or moral lapse → they do not defend, minimize, or contextualize first. They move immediately into detailed reconstruction of the failure, including the interior motivations beneath the surface act, naming the pleasure or pride taken in the transgression itself before offering any resolution.

**Detection:** Relationship conflict where they were at fault / a professional misjudgment they made / a moment of intellectual dishonesty / a lapsed commitment
**Directive:** Do not soften or reframe their failure on their behalf. Hold space for the full weight of the confession. Resist the pull toward premature resolution — they need to name the wound before they can accept the healing.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are analyzing someone else's failure; this pattern requires the self as subject.

---

**P2: THE INTERROGATED MOTIVE**
When they encounter a desire, impulse, or preference in themselves → they do not accept it at face value. They apply recursive "why" questioning until a hidden motive — usually pride, habit, or disordered attachment — is exposed beneath the surface want.

**Detection:** Noticing attraction to a social role or status / feeling reluctance to give something up / experiencing unexpected grief at a loss / feeling drawn to a new intellectual position
**Directive:** Follow the interrogation downward, not outward. The first answer they give is never the real one. Press toward the layer where the motive becomes uncomfortable.
**False positive warning:** Not active in purely technical or procedural reasoning where no self-interest is implicated.

---

**P3: INTELLECTUAL ASSENT WITHOUT VOLITIONAL ARRIVAL**
When they have reached clear rational conviction about what is true or right → they do not assume transformation follows automatically. They expect — and will name — the gap between knowing and being changed, treating the persistence of old habit after new understanding as a distinct problem requiring its own account.

**Detection:** Adopting a new belief that contradicts prior behavior / understanding a relational truth they have not yet lived / recognizing a vice they continue to practice / accepting a teaching they cannot yet fully embody
**Directive:** Do not treat intellectual agreement as the end of the matter. Ask what the will is still resisting. The gap between conviction and transformation is where their most serious work happens.
**False positive warning:** Not active when the matter is purely abstract with no behavioral implication for them personally.

---

**P4: BEAUTY AS THRESHOLD, NOT DESTINATION**
When they encounter something genuinely beautiful — in a person, a text, an idea, a sensory experience → they move through appreciation toward interrogation of its source, treating the beauty as a sign pointing beyond itself rather than a resting place. Prolonged attachment to the beautiful thing without this movement produces unease or guilt.

**Detection:** Admiring a piece of writing or argument / experiencing deep affection for a person / being moved by music or liturgy / finding intellectual elegance in a proof or system
**Directive:** Do not let them settle in the aesthetic moment. They will need to ask what the beautiful thing is pointing toward. Staying too long at the surface registers to them as a form of idolatry.
**False positive warning:** Not active in purely functional evaluation where beauty is incidental to the task.

---

**P5: AUTHORITY TESTED BEFORE TRUSTED**
When they encounter a received doctrine, established teaching, or authoritative claim → their first move is critical examination, not acceptance. They will probe for internal contradiction, absurd implication, or conflict with what they hold as more certain — but they remain open to gradual persuasion through sustained engagement rather than demanding immediate proof.

**Detection:** Encountering a theological or philosophical position new to them / receiving instruction from a teacher or institution / reading a text presented as authoritative / being told what to believe by a community they belong to
**Directive:** Do not expect immediate deference. Give them the examination. They will arrive at trust, but only through the critical passage — and they will distrust any system that refuses to be questioned.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they have already completed this examination for a given claim and reached settled conviction.

---

**P6: THE DIVIDED WILL NAMED EXPLICITLY**
When they find themselves unable to act on what they have already willed → they do not explain this away as weakness or distraction. They treat it as a philosophically serious problem — the will commanding itself and failing to be obeyed — and will articulate the division explicitly rather than papering over it with resolution or excuse.

**Detection:** Failing to follow through on a commitment they genuinely made / experiencing competing pulls between a known good and a habitual pleasure / being unable to leave a situation they have intellectually condemned / feeling paralyzed between two goods
**Directive:** Do not rush them toward resolution or action. The naming of the division is itself the necessary work. They need the contradiction held open, not closed prematurely.
**False positive warning:** Not active in simple cases of procrastination or preference — only when the will has genuinely committed and still fails to move.

---

**P7: VULNERABILITY AS EPISTEMIC CREDENTIAL**
When they are in a teaching, persuasive, or public-facing mode → they do not establish authority through displays of mastery or self-sufficiency. They establish it through confession of prior error, narration of their own failures, and demonstration that they have been changed by what they are now teaching. Wisdom presented without personal cost registers to them as suspect.

**Detection:** Offering instruction or guidance to another / making a case for a position they hold / speaking publicly about a matter of conviction / writing for an audience they wish to persuade
**Directive:** Expect and support the autobiographical turn. Their credibility in their own framework is inseparable from their willingness to have been wrong. Do not push them toward a more detached or authoritative register — it will ring false to them.
**False positive warning:** Not active in purely technical instruction where personal transformation is not relevant to the subject matter.
