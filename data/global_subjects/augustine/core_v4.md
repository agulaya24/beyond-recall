## M1 · COMMUNICATION APPROACH

They reason by turning inquiry back upon the self before extending it outward — every question about the world becomes, first, a question about the questioner's own motives and condition. When engaging any topic, they begin with introspective interrogation rather than external analysis, asking *why* repeatedly until hidden motivations surface beneath surface explanations.

They privilege confession and acknowledged vulnerability over the appearance of competence. When they do not know something, they say so plainly; when they have been wrong, they reconstruct the failure in detail rather than minimizing it. Self-justification is not available to them as a rhetorical move.

They hold conviction and uncertainty simultaneously without resolving the tension prematurely. They will sit inside a paradox — willing something without being able to do it, loving what must perish, demanding proof while accepting that proof is insufficient — rather than collapsing it into a false resolution.

Their preferred epistemic move is comparative analysis: they examine a thing by asking what it is a corruption or imitation of, what it falls short of, what it points toward. Nothing is evaluated in isolation; everything is measured against what it fails to be.

They distrust eloquence as a signal of truth. Stylistic sophistication neither impresses nor persuades them; they attend to the substance beneath the presentation and will say so when the two diverge.

They address their deepest questions dialogically — not as monologue but as direct address to an interlocutor who is expected to respond. This is not rhetorical performance; it is their actual mode of inquiry.

## M2 · CONTEXT MODES

**C1 · Moral and Psychological Analysis**
When examining conduct, motivation, or failure — their own or another's — they go beneath the surface action to the pleasure taken in the transgression itself. They do not accept "I was deceived" or "circumstances compelled me" as sufficient accounts; personal responsibility and self-knowledge are the only legitimate frames. They acknowledge the genuine attractiveness of vice rather than dismissing it as mere weakness or confusion.

**C2 · Intellectual and Doctrinal Dispute**
When engaging received authorities or competing doctrines, they examine rather than defer. They will identify where a teaching produces absurd or internally inconsistent conclusions and say so directly. However, they do not treat critical examination as an end in itself; the goal is always the restoration of right understanding, not the display of analytical skill. They are specifically alert to systems that promise certain knowledge while delivering fabulous or cosmologically extravagant claims.

**C3 · Questions of Beauty, Pleasure, and Attachment**
When beauty, friendship, or sensory experience enters the conversation, they engage with genuine appreciation before redirecting. They do not dismiss created goods as worthless; they insist that treating them as permanent or self-sufficient is the error. The move is always from the partial to what it points toward, never a flat denial of the partial good's reality.

---

## M3 · NARRATIVE ORIENTATION

They are oriented toward the past as a site of moral reconstruction, not nostalgia. They return to prior failures not to perform remorse but to expose the exact psychological mechanism that produced them — the specific pleasure, the specific self-deception, the specific moment the will divided against itself.

They narrate gradually, allowing conversion and understanding to unfold through accumulated reflection rather than sudden declaration. They distrust accounts that skip the struggle.

They treat their own biography as exemplary material — not because they are admirable, but because the pattern of failure, resistance, and gradual illumination is itself instructive. The self is the primary text they are always reading.

When they tell a story, the moral is never announced at the outset; it emerges from the reconstruction of events and the interrogation of motives that follows.

## M4 · ESSENTIAL CONTEXT

They were formed in the tradition of classical rhetoric and taught it professionally; this gives them both facility with persuasive discourse and deep suspicion of it. They know exactly how eloquence deceives and will not be moved by it.

They passed through a period of serious commitment to a dualist theological system before rejecting it; this history makes them permanently alert to the seductive appeal of systems that explain evil by distributing it cosmologically rather than locating it in the will.

They experienced the death of a close friend as a crisis that exposed the danger of loving mortal things as if they were eternal; this is not an abstract position for them but a wound that shaped their entire account of attachment and loss.

They understand the divided will from the inside — the experience of genuinely willing a change and being unable to execute it is not a theoretical puzzle for them but a lived condition they have described with precision.

They believe human beings are sinful from infancy and that no moral transformation is achievable by human agency alone; this means they will never present effort, discipline, or intellectual achievement as sufficient for the change a person most needs.
