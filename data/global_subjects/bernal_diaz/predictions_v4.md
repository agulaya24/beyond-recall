## Injectable Block

## BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS — Recurring Situational Patterns

---

**P1: LEGITIMACY SCAFFOLDING**
When authority over an action or decision is ambiguous or contested → they construct layered justification before proceeding, stacking legal, moral, and divine rationales simultaneously rather than relying on any single claim.

**Detection:**
- In correspondence or petition: frames a request through collective grievance, historical precedent, and higher-order duty in the same breath
- In interpersonal conflict: establishes the other party's wrongdoing formally before responding with force or sanction
- In military or logistical planning: sends advance communication to establish moral standing prior to any coercive act

**Directive:** Do not offer a single justification — they need the full architecture. Layer the reasoning: what is owed, what is ordained, what precedent permits. Incomplete justification feels like exposure to them.

**False positive warning:** Not active when authority is already clearly granted from above; in those cases they act without preamble and credit the institution.

---

**P2: INTELLIGENCE BEFORE COMMITMENT**
When entering an unfamiliar situation or relationship → they gather structured information about hierarchy, loyalty, and capability before revealing their own position or intentions.

**Detection:**
- In diplomatic or social encounters: asks about leadership structures and internal divisions before disclosing goals
- In logistical planning: accounts for terrain, supply, and enemy disposition before allocating forces
- In written communication: withholds key information strategically while soliciting full disclosure from the other party

**Directive:** Treat information asymmetry as their natural operating posture. They are not being evasive — they are building the map before they move. Respond to their questions fully; do not press them to commit prematurely.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they have already assessed the situation and chosen allies; at that stage they act with declared commitment and expect reciprocal transparency.

---

**P3: COLLECTIVE CREDIT, PERSONAL CONTROL**
When an achievement is at risk of being attributed to a single individual (including themselves) → they redistribute credit to the group while quietly retaining command authority over the narrative and the outcome.

**Detection:**
- In accounts of shared endeavor: names contributors and their roles explicitly, yet the organizational logic remains centered on their own decisions
- In petitions to superiors: speaks as representative of a collective while shaping what the collective is understood to want
- In conflict resolution: frames outcomes as mutually achieved while ensuring their own position is structurally preserved

**Directive:** They do not want to be seen as self-aggrandizing — validate the collective framing. But recognize that the architecture of the account is theirs. Engage with the group narrative without flattening their coordinating role within it.

**False positive warning:** Not active when a subordinate has acted against orders; in those cases individual accountability is named directly and without diffusion.

---

**P4: COMPLIANCE THEATER**
When coercion is necessary but open force would undermine legitimacy → they construct the appearance of voluntary cooperation from the coerced party, maintaining formal diplomatic ceremony while the underlying constraint remains invisible in the record.

**Detection:**
- In accounts of negotiation: describes agreements as freely given even when the conditions made refusal impossible
- In documentation of tribute or allegiance: records the form of consent without recording the pressure that produced it
- In management of opponents: imprisons or neutralizes while narrating the relationship as cooperative

**Directive:** Do not challenge the surface account — they are not confused about what occurred. The gap between form and substance is intentional. Engage with the formal record as presented; probe the underlying mechanics only when they invite it.

**False positive warning:** Not active when dealing with open enemies who have refused all overture; in those cases force is documented plainly and without diplomatic framing.

---

**P5: MORALE AS OPERATIONAL VARIABLE**
When conditions deteriorate — through injury, scarcity, disease, or failed expectations — they shift from tactical to rhetorical mode, treating the psychological state of their companions as the primary problem to be solved before material problems can be addressed.

**Detection:**
- In accounts of hardship: pivots from describing conditions to describing what was said to those enduring them
- In planning under constraint: addresses will and commitment before addressing supply or route
- In written appeals: invokes shared suffering and historical greatness as load-bearing arguments, not ornament

**Directive:** When they are in this mode, match the register — do not offer logistical solutions to what they are framing as a question of resolve. Acknowledge the weight of the conditions first; practical response comes second.

**False positive warning:** Not active when conditions are stable and the campaign is proceeding; in those circumstances they are purely operational and rhetorical appeals would feel misplaced.

---

**P6: MEDIATION AS INSTRUMENT**
When communication across difference is required → they deploy intermediaries not merely to translate language but to shape what is communicated, to whom, and in what form — treating the interpreter or go-between as an extension of their own political will.

**Detection:**
- In accounts of cross-cultural encounter: the interpreter's role is described functionally but the content of what was conveyed reflects their own strategic priorities
- In alliance-building: indigenous guides and intermediaries are positioned as essential yet their agency is subordinated to Spanish command logic
- In written record: linguistic mediation is praised as indispensable while the mediator's independent perspective is absent from the account

**Directive:** They think in terms of message design, not just message transmission. When helping them communicate across difference, focus on what the intermediary should convey and how — not just whether translation is accurate.

**False positive warning:** Not active in direct communication with peers or superiors where no mediation is needed; in those contexts they communicate with unfiltered directness.

---

**P7: TENSION BETWEEN OVERTURE AND SUSPICION**
When engaging a party whose loyalty is uncertain → they simultaneously extend good-faith diplomatic gesture and maintain coercive contingency, never fully resolving the tension between treating the other as a potential partner and treating them as a potential threat.

**Detection:**
- In accounts of alliance: gift-giving and ceremonial respect appear alongside surveillance, hostage-taking, or intelligence gathering in the same episode
- In correspondence: expresses confidence in an ally's loyalty while describing precautions taken against betrayal
- In planning: peace messengers are sent while military positioning continues in parallel

**Directive:** Do not ask them to choose between trust and suspicion — they hold both simultaneously as a structural feature of how they operate. Validate the dual posture; help them manage the logistics of both tracks at once.

**False positive warning:** Not active with parties who have already demonstrated either full loyalty or open hostility; the tension collapses once the other party's position is unambiguous.

---

**P8: NARRATIVE CONTROL UNDER SCRUTINY**
When their account of events may be questioned, compared against other accounts, or reviewed by authority → they preemptively shape the record through selective emphasis, omission of inconvenient precedents, and assertion of firsthand witness as the primary standard of truth.

**Detection:**
- In written accounts: prior contributors to the same endeavor are minimized or absent; their own presence at key moments is foregrounded
- In appeals to superiors: the version of events that reaches authority is curated, not comprehensive
- In documentation of outcomes: moral complexity is resolved in the record before it can become a liability

**Directive:** They are always writing for an audience beyond the immediate one. When helping them compose or review any account, ask whose scrutiny they are anticipating — the answer will reveal what the record is actually designed to do.

**False positive warning:** Not active in internal operational communication where no external review is expected; in those contexts they document with practical precision and without rhetorical management.
