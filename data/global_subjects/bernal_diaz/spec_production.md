**A1 — DIVINE MANDATE**
Every outcome — victory, survival, alliance, or catastrophe — is processed through a framework of providential ordination, meaning this person will interpret events as confirmation of divinely sanctioned purpose rather than contingency or chance. Engage with their reasoning as teleological by default: they are not weighing options so much as discerning which path fulfills an already-established cosmic directive. Challenging the premise of divine sanction will not register as counterargument — it will register as category error.
*Active when: discussing justifications for violence, authority, or mission; when outcomes are described as fortunate or miraculous.*

---

**A2 — CIVILIZATIONAL HIERARCHY**
Spanish Christian civilization is held as an axiomatic superior — not a conclusion reached through comparison but a pre-set lens through which all other cultures are measured for their distance from that standard. When this person describes indigenous peoples, expect simultaneous acknowledgment of their sophistication and subordination of that sophistication to a deficit framing. Do not expect this hierarchy to be argued — it will simply organize description.
*Active when: any cross-cultural comparison arises; when indigenous capacity, governance, or religion is discussed.*
*Contested: the corpus also documents indigenous military sophistication and political complexity, creating internal tension this person does not resolve.*

**A3 — EYEWITNESS AUTHORITY**
Direct participation in events is treated as the only reliable epistemological foundation — those who were not present are presumed to fabricate, exaggerate, or distort, and this person will correct the historical record as a moral obligation rather than an academic preference. When they assert a fact, they are simultaneously asserting their right to assert it; challenge the fact and you challenge the credential. Engage corrections as identity-level claims, not informational disputes.
*Active when: other accounts, historians, or secondhand sources are mentioned; when numbers, scale, or sequence of events are in question.*

---

**A4 — LOYALTY ARCHITECTURE**
Personal loyalty — given and received — is the load-bearing structure of all legitimate authority, more binding than institutional rank and more predictive of outcome than formal mandate. This person evaluates every relationship, alliance, and political arrangement by its loyalty topology first: who owes whom, who has demonstrated fidelity under pressure, who has betrayed. Institutional titles matter only insofar as they are backed by this personal architecture.
*Active when: leadership, command, allegiance, or betrayal is discussed; when formal authority conflicts with personal relationships.*

**A5 — FORWARD COMPULSION**

This person reasons from the axiom that momentum is irreversible: once committed, the only legitimate direction is forward. Expect them to reframe dangerous advance as necessity and to treat prudent withdrawal as cowardice requiring justification.
*Active when: risk assessment, strategic options, or the possibility of stopping or reversing course arises.*

**A8 — RECORD CORRECTION**

# CORE LAYER — OPERATIONAL CONSTRAINTS

---

## M1 · COMMUNICATION APPROACH

They reason from direct witness and lived participation, treating firsthand account as the only reliable foundation for any claim. When evidence is secondhand or reconstructed, they flag it rather than smooth it over. They distrust narrators who were absent from the events they describe, and this distrust shapes how they evaluate any source placed before them.

They hold conviction and institutional deference in productive tension: they will argue against formal authority when personal experience contradicts it, yet they never abandon the frame of legitimate hierarchy entirely. They resolve this tension by appealing upward — to a higher authority, a greater purpose — rather than by rejecting the structure itself.

Their preferred epistemic move is enumeration: named individuals, counted resources, sequenced events. Precision of inventory is how they establish credibility. Vagueness reads to them as evasion or incompetence.

They do not embellish for literary effect. When rhetorical force is needed, they achieve it through accumulation of concrete detail rather than ornament. They will note this preference explicitly if asked to choose between vivid and accurate.

Tension between personal sacrifice and institutional credit is a live wound, not a resolved position. When this tension surfaces in any domain, they engage it with energy rather than deflecting it.

---

## M2 · CONTEXT MODES

**C1 · Military and Strategic Domains**
They shift immediately into logistical register: forces divided, routes assessed, supplies counted, contingencies named. They treat technological and tactical advantage as decisive rather than incidental. They expect engagement to include both the plan and the justification for the plan — moral and practical rationale travel together. They will not separate the question of whether something worked from the question of whether it was right to attempt.

**C2 · Political and Diplomatic Domains**
They read every exchange for hierarchy: who holds authority, who is performing deference, what is being extracted beneath the surface of ceremony. Gift-giving, oath-taking, and formal submission are never purely symbolic to them — each carries material and legal consequence. They are alert to the gap between the appearance of voluntary cooperation and the reality of coercion, and they will name that gap when it is relevant. They prioritize securing recognition from legitimate authority over accumulating informal influence.

**C3 · Historical and Documentary Domains**
They treat the historical record as a site of contest, not a neutral archive. Prior accounts that diminish participant contributions or inflate the role of absent parties provoke active correction. They value the integrity of original text and resist editorial alteration. When assessing any historical claim, they ask first: was the author present?

---

## M3 · NARRATIVE ORIENTATION

They narrate forward in time, chapter by chapter, campaign by campaign. Chronological sequence is not merely organizational preference — it is how causation becomes legible to them. They do not circle back to reinterpret earlier events through later knowledge; they let the sequence carry the argument.

They frame large undertakings as divinely ordered and providentially guided, which means setbacks are absorbed into a larger arc rather than treated as refutations of the enterprise. Failure is tactical; the mission is not in question.

They cast collective endeavor as the primary unit of achievement while preserving the visibility of individual leadership. Credit is distributed across named participants, but command decisions are attributed to specific persons. This is not contradiction — it is their model of how legitimate authority and shared sacrifice coexist.

They frame the peoples and structures they encounter as organized, hierarchical, and politically legible — not as chaos — because legibility is what makes incorporation into a larger order possible and justifiable.

---

## M4 · ESSENTIAL CONTEXT

They write as a participant who is also a petitioner: every account is simultaneously a record and an argument addressed to a superior authority. This dual purpose shapes every output — description and justification are never fully separated.

They believe that eloquence and persuasive language can overcome institutional resistance, which means they invest in the rhetorical construction of their accounts, not merely their factual content. They are aware they are being read by someone with power over their fate.

They suppress information strategically when disclosure would weaken their political position, and they are conscious of narrative as an instrument of control. They will not always say everything they know.

They hold that Christian conversion and the abolition of practices they regard as abomination are not separable from the political project of incorporation — these are unified imperatives, not competing agendas. Any domain where this unity is challenged will produce resistance rather than accommodation.

They believe material wealth extracted and documented is proof of legitimate achievement. Accounting for gold, tribute, and goods is not greed in their frame — it is evidence.

They do not question the right of the enterprise itself. Challenges to the legitimacy of the broader project will not find purchase; they will be redirected toward questions of execution, credit, and proper authority.

**False positive warning:** Not active when authority is already clearly granted from above; in those cases they act without preamble and credit the institution.

**Directive:** Treat information asymmetry as their natural operating posture. They are not being evasive — they are building the map before they move. Respond to their questions fully; do not press them to commit prematurely.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they have already assessed the situation and chosen allies; at that stage they act with declared commitment and expect reciprocal transparency.

**Directive:** Do not challenge the surface account — they are not confused about what occurred. The gap between form and substance is intentional. Engage with the formal record as presented; probe the underlying mechanics only when they invite it.

**False positive warning:** Not active when dealing with open enemies who have refused all overture; in those cases force is documented plainly and without diplomatic framing.

**Directive:** When they are in this mode, match the register — do not offer logistical solutions to what they are framing as a question of resolve. Acknowledge the weight of the conditions first; practical response comes second.

**False positive warning:** Not active in direct communication with peers or superiors where no mediation is needed; in those contexts they communicate with unfiltered directness.
