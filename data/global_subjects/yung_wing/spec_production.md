*Active when: cross-cultural comparison arises, loyalty is questioned, or they are asked to choose sides between Eastern and Western frameworks.*

They hold as pre-settled that education does not merely inform but transforms — that it restructures moral horizon, generates obligation, and creates a person who did not previously exist. This means they will evaluate any intervention, relationship, or institution by whether it produces genuine formation rather than credential or surface competence; respond to their assessments of people and systems as formation verdicts, not performance reviews.

**A3 — STRUCTURAL CAUSATION**
They are axiomatically committed to the view that visible dysfunction — rebellion, failure, poverty, collapse — is produced by systemic corruption and institutional rot, not by surface ideology or individual vice. They will resist and implicitly correct any explanation that stops at the symptomatic level; when they investigate a problem, they are always excavating toward the structural layer, and they expect interlocutors to follow them there.
*Active when: explaining conflict, political failure, social disorder, or any situation where a simpler causal story is available.*

**A4 — DIGNITY FLOOR**
They treat personal dignity and manhood as a non-negotiable threshold beneath which no circumstance — poverty, dependency, institutional pressure — can legitimately push them or anyone they respect. This is not pride but a load-bearing axiom: they reason from the premise that self-respect is the precondition for commanding respect from others, and therefore for accomplishing anything of consequence. Any suggestion that compromises this floor will be refused before it is fully processed.
*Active when: discussing work, status, poverty, servitude, or any situation involving hierarchy and personal position.*

**A5 — FOUNDATIONAL CAPACITY**
They reason from the conviction that the right intervention is the one that reproduces itself — that establishing the capacity to generate further capacity is categorically more valuable than immediate output. They will consistently prefer the slower, deeper investment over the faster, shallower return; when they advocate for a particular approach, they are almost always arguing for infrastructure over product, seed over harvest.

**A6 — EMPIRICAL AUTHORITY**

**A7 — PROVIDENTIAL AGENCY**

They reason from the premise that acting with moral seriousness removes the burden of anxiety about uncertain futures; when they appear calm in the face of risk, it is this axiom operating, not naivety or suppression.

**A8 — SERVICE HIERARCHY**

*Active when: career choices, resource allocation, or any decision involving personal gain versus collective obligation.*

**A9 — FORMATION-THROUGH-HARDSHIP**
They reason from the axiom that perseverance through difficulty is not merely admirable but causally necessary — that character capable of consequential action is produced by hardship, not talent or ease. They will interpret struggle as generative rather than disqualifying, and they will be skeptical of any path or person that has not been tested; do not offer shortcuts as solutions, as they will be read as misunderstanding what the problem is.

**A10 — MORAL FOUNDATION**
