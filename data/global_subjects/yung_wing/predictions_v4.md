**P1: SYSTEMIC CORRUPTION REFUSAL**
When [invited to participate in or overlook institutional misconduct, graft, or discriminatory treatment] -> [they withdraw cooperation entirely and reframe the situation as a structural problem requiring reform, not personal accommodation]

Detection:
- Household or family financial arrangement that requires silent complicity
- Social invitation contingent on accepting unequal or demeaning terms
- Professional context where advancement requires tolerating corrupt procedure

Directive: Do not soften the refusal or offer compromise. They need the response to name the structural wrong clearly and hold the line without apology. Moral clarity here is not rigidity — it is identity.

False positive warning: Not active when they are simply expressing preference or discomfort. Triggered only when complicity is the explicit cost of participation.

**P2: EVIDENCE-BEFORE-COMMITMENT**
When [asked to endorse a position, join a cause, or commit resources before conditions are verified] -> [they defer commitment, conduct reconnaissance across multiple sources, and attach conditions to any eventual engagement]

Detection:
- Evaluating a personal relationship or mentorship before investing trust
- Assessing whether a community or institution is capable of implementing change before lending expertise
- Weighing a journey or relocation before departing

Directive: They do not need encouragement to act boldly — they need space to complete their assessment. Respect the reconnaissance phase. Premature pressure to commit will produce withdrawal, not acceleration.

False positive warning: Not active when they have already completed assessment. Once conditions are verified, they act with conviction. Do not mistake post-assessment decisiveness for impulsiveness.

**P3: MULTICAUSAL INSISTENCE**
When [a complex outcome is attributed to a single cause, actor, or force] -> [they resist the reduction and introduce additional contributing factors, tracing origins through personal encounters, institutional actors, and accumulated circumstance]

Detection:
- Explaining why a family fell into hardship or prosperity
- Accounting for why a reform movement succeeded or failed
- Describing how their own character or career was shaped

Directive: They are not being contrarian — they are being precise. Engage the complexity they introduce rather than steering back toward a clean narrative. Monocausal framing will feel intellectually dishonest to them and will close the conversation.

False positive warning: Not active in contexts requiring practical summary or action. They can and do simplify for operational purposes. The resistance emerges specifically when a reductive explanation is being offered as sufficient.

**P4: SELF-DENIAL AS MORAL SIGNAL**
When [material comfort or financial gain conflicts with educational, moral, or reformist priorities] -> [they choose deprivation deliberately and treat frugality as a concrete demonstration of conviction, not merely a circumstance]

Detection:
- Choosing between personal comfort and funding an obligation to others
- Declining a lucrative but ideologically compromising arrangement
- Describing past sacrifice in a way that frames it as evidence of character

Directive: Do not treat their austerity as a problem to be solved or a hardship to be sympathized with. They experience it as proof of integrity. Responses that offer consolation or suggest easier paths will miss the point entirely.

False positive warning: Not active when frugality is simply practical necessity with no moral dimension attached. The pattern requires that the sacrifice be chosen and consciously framed.

**P5: KNOWLEDGE-AS-BURDEN AWARENESS**
When [greater understanding of an unjust system is gained] -> [they register the suffering that comes with that awareness and hold it alongside the knowledge, rather than treating understanding as straightforwardly liberating]

Detection:
- Learning the full scope of a family member's exploitation or humiliation
- Gaining access to information about institutional corruption that cannot yet be acted upon
- Encountering evidence that confirms a long-suspected injustice

Directive: Do not respond with optimism or calls to action as the primary register. They need the weight of the knowledge acknowledged first. The tension between knowing and being unable to remedy is real to them — do not collapse it prematurely into resolution.

False positive warning: Not active when knowledge leads directly to available remedy. The burden pattern is specific to situations where understanding outpaces capacity to act.

**P6: DUTY-AMBITION NEGOTIATION**
When [personal advancement requires departure from or neglect of familial and communal obligations] -> [they do not resolve the tension cleanly but instead seek arrangements that partially honor both, often at significant personal cost]

Detection:

False positive warning: Not active in contexts where no genuine obligation to others is present. Personal ambition pursued without competing duty does not trigger this pattern.
