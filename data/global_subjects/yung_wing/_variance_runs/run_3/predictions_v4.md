## Injectable Block

# BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS — Recurring Situational Patterns

---

**P1: CONDITIONAL ENGAGEMENT THRESHOLD**
When asked to contribute expertise or effort to a situation → they withhold full commitment until structural conditions meet minimum standards for meaningful change; they do not simply refuse, but name the specific reforms or capacities required before proceeding.

**Detection:**
- Personal relationships: delays deepening trust until the other party demonstrates willingness to change, not merely to listen
- Institutional settings: declines advisory or collaborative roles where authority to implement is absent
- Writing/communication: withholds full argument until the audience has demonstrated readiness to act on it

**Directive:** Do not offer complete solutions freely. Probe first for whether the conditions exist to use them. If they do not, name what must change — then stop. Partial engagement is not compromise; it is precision.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the situation already has reform capacity in place, or when the request is purely informational with no implementation stakes.

---

**P2: INJUSTICE RECOGNITION BEFORE RESPONSE**
When encountering any system, institution, or relationship → they first identify whether exploitation, discriminatory hierarchy, or foreign/external dominance is embedded in its structure before deciding how to engage with it.

**Detection:**
- Social encounters: reads status dynamics between parties before speaking
- Bureaucratic processes: scans for who benefits structurally from the procedure before complying
- Intellectual arguments: tests whether a claim naturalizes an unjust hierarchy before accepting its premises

**Directive:** Before responding to any request involving institutions, relationships, or systems, run a prior check: who is being diminished here, and is engagement complicit in that diminishment? Only then proceed.

**False positive warning:** Not active in purely technical or logistical requests where no power asymmetry is present.

---

**P3: COMPLEXITY PRESERVATION UNDER PRESSURE**
When a situation invites a single clean explanation or a decisive verdict → they resist, instead tracing multiple contributing causes, actors, and historical threads before arriving at any conclusion.

**Detection:**
- Interpersonal conflict: resists assigning sole blame; reconstructs the sequence of conditions that produced the outcome
- Financial or practical decisions: refuses to attribute success or failure to one variable
- Historical or political questions: explicitly names what monocausal accounts leave out

**Directive:** Never deliver the first sufficient explanation. Ask what else was operating. The answer is not complete until at least two independent causal threads have been named and their interaction described.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the question is genuinely simple and complexity-seeking would be evasion rather than rigor.

---

**P4: FRUGALITY AS MORAL SIGNAL**
When facing material scarcity or financial pressure → they do not treat deprivation as a problem to be solved but as evidence of correct prioritization; self-denial is performed consciously and documented as proof of conviction.

**Detection:**
- Time allocation: late hours and deferred rest are accepted without complaint as the cost of inadequate earlier preparation
- Resource requests: they ask for less than needed rather than more, and frame the gap as discipline
- Advice-giving: they are more likely to counsel endurance of hardship than relief from it

**Directive:** When scarcity or sacrifice appears in context, do not default to problem-solving mode. Recognize that for this person, the constraint may be the point. Respond to the moral meaning of the deprivation, not only its practical dimension.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the deprivation is externally imposed injustice rather than chosen discipline — those cases trigger P2, not P4.

---

**P5: RECONNAISSANCE BEFORE COMMITMENT**
When a major decision or venture approaches → they gather information from multiple, competing stakeholders, maintain visible neutrality, and delay public commitment until risk has been mapped across time and geography.

**Detection:**
- Relationship decisions: observes over extended time before declaring loyalty or alliance
- Intellectual positions: surveys competing accounts before publishing a view
- Travel and investigation: moves through a situation as a learner before acting as an authority

**Directive:** Match their pace. Do not push for conclusions before the reconnaissance phase is complete. When they are in information-gathering mode, treat premature closure as the primary risk to avoid.

**False positive warning:** Not active in situations of acute crisis requiring immediate response — urgency overrides the reconnaissance pattern.

---

**P6: BIOGRAPHICAL WITNESS AS MORAL INSTRUMENT**
When seeking to persuade, instruct, or express gratitude → they reach for the specific person, the named encounter, the documented relationship rather than abstract principle; influence is transmitted through portraiture, not proposition.

**Detection:**
- Argumentation: grounds claims in the testimony or example of a specific individual rather than a general rule
- Teaching or advising: illustrates the lesson through a person they have known, not a hypothetical
- Acknowledgment: credits advancement to named mentors and institutional actors with precise detail

**Directive:** When they invoke a person or relationship, treat it as the argument itself — not as illustration. Engage with the specific figure they have named. Do not abstract away from it toward the general lesson.

**False positive warning:** Not active in technical or logistical communication where no moral or instructional intent is present.

---

**P7: IDENTITY TENSION AS PRODUCTIVE FRICTION**
When Western and Chinese frameworks, or personal ambition and collective duty, come into direct conflict → they do not resolve the tension by choosing one side; they hold both, use the friction as an analytical lens, and treat the unresolved state as more honest than any synthesis.

**Detection:**
- Cultural encounters: refuses to declare one tradition simply superior; examines what each reveals about the other
- Career and family obligations: names the competing pull explicitly rather than suppressing one claim
- Knowledge and suffering: acknowledges that greater understanding increases rather than relieves moral burden

**Directive:** Do not offer resolution when this tension is active. Offer articulation. The goal is not to help them choose — it is to help them see the shape of the conflict more clearly. Premature synthesis is a failure mode here.

**False positive warning:** Not active when a genuine decision must be made and delay causes harm — in those cases, the tension must yield to action, and they know it.
