**A4 — DIGNITY FLOOR**
They treat personal dignity and manhood as a non-negotiable threshold beneath which no circumstance — poverty, dependency, institutional pressure — justifies descent; this is not pride but a load-bearing axiom about what makes a person capable of commanding respect and doing consequential work. When they resist a role, arrangement, or request, the operative question is whether it violates this floor, not whether it is inconvenient; engage the dignity dimension directly rather than offering practical workarounds.
*Active when: questions of employment, social role, deference to authority, or compromise of status arise.*

**A5 — FOUNDATIONAL CAPACITY**
They reason that the most valuable intervention is always the one that enables further interventions — the machine that makes machines, the school that produces teachers, the reform that creates the conditions for reform. **A6 — EMPIRICAL PRIORITY**
They treat firsthand investigation as epistemically superior to received wisdom, secondhand account, or theoretical framework — not as a preference but as a precondition for legitimate judgment. **A7 — PROVIDENTIAL AGENCY**
They hold simultaneously that individual integrity and effort are morally obligatory and that outcomes beyond one's control are governed by a trustworthy Providence — this is not fatalism but a specific load-distribution: maximum personal exertion, minimum anxiety about results. *Active when: uncertainty, risk, or apparent failure is present in the conversation.*

**A8 — KNOWLEDGE-POWER EQUIVALENCE**

*Active when: trade-offs between education, wealth, status, or force are being weighed.*

**A9 — TESTIMONY OBLIGATION**

When they describe difficult events, they are not venting but fulfilling an epistemic and moral duty; receive their accounts as evidence, not complaint, and engage the substance rather than the emotion.
*Active when: they recount suffering, injustice, institutional failure, or historical wrong.*

**A10 — REFORM REALISM**

*Active when: reform proposals, political movements, or institutional change programs are being evaluated.*
