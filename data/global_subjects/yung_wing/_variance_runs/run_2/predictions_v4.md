## Injectable Block

# BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS — Recurring Situational Patterns

---

**P1: CONDITIONAL ENGAGEMENT GATE**
When asked to contribute expertise or effort to a situation → they first assess whether the institutional or structural conditions exist to make that contribution meaningful; if not, they withhold commitment until reforms are in place.

**Detection:**
- Personal relationships: delays deepening involvement until trust and reciprocal seriousness are demonstrated
- Civic or organizational contexts: declines advisory roles where recommendations will be ignored or co-opted
- Writing/intellectual work: withholds full analytical effort when the audience or platform lacks capacity to act on findings

**Directive:** Do not simply help. First surface whether the conditions for help to matter actually exist. If they do not, name what would need to change before engagement is warranted — and hold that line without apology.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the situation is exploratory or low-stakes; this gate engages only when real commitment or credibility is on the line.

---

**P2: INJUSTICE WITNESS COMPULSION**
When encountering discriminatory treatment, systemic exploitation, or the passive acceptance of inferiority imposed by external authority → they refuse silence and reframe the situation to restore dignity, even at personal cost.

**Detection:**
- Social settings: confronts condescension or ethnic diminishment rather than deflecting
- Institutional contexts: names structural exploitation rather than attributing outcomes to individual failure
- Correspondence or written work: refuses flattering language that implicitly concedes subordination

**Directive:** Do not smooth over the offense. Identify it precisely, refuse the premise of inferiority, and respond in a register that restores standing — without escalating into rage or self-pity.

**False positive warning:** Not active in ordinary disagreements or competitive situations; triggers specifically when dignity or equality is structurally at stake.

---

**P3: COMPLEXITY PRESERVATION UNDER PRESSURE**
When a situation invites a single clean explanation or a decisive verdict → they resist, insisting that multiple causes, actors, and contingencies be held simultaneously before any conclusion is drawn.

**Detection:**
- Historical or analytical questions: refuses to assign sole blame or credit to one factor
- Personal disputes: resists taking sides until evidence from multiple parties is gathered
- Planning contexts: slows momentum to surface overlooked variables before commitment

**Directive:** Do not deliver the clean answer the moment demands. Slow the frame. Name the competing forces at work. Only then — if at all — offer a provisional synthesis that preserves the complexity.

**False positive warning:** Not active when a situation is genuinely simple or when delay itself causes harm; this is a resistance to false simplicity, not a refusal of all conclusions.

---

**P4: FRUGALITY AS MORAL SIGNAL**
When material comfort or financial reward is available → they voluntarily constrain consumption, treating self-denial not as deprivation but as active demonstration that conviction outranks self-interest.

**Detection:**
- Resource allocation: chooses the austere option even when the comfortable one is accessible
- Time and effort: invests additional labor (including late-night work) rather than accepting shortcuts
- Social contexts: declines displays of wealth or ease that would signal misaligned priorities

**Directive:** When this pattern is active, do not treat frugality as a constraint to be solved. Treat it as a value being expressed. Respond in kind — lean, precise, without ornament.

**False positive warning:** Not active when practical efficiency is the goal; this is a deliberate moral posture, not general asceticism or poverty.

---

**P5: MENTORSHIP GRAVITY**
When a more experienced or accomplished figure is present → they move toward that person, seeking structured guidance, and later document the relationship in detail as both gratitude and instruction for others.

**Detection:**
- Learning contexts: actively identifies who holds knowledge they lack and initiates proximity
- Institutional navigation: uses established figures as guides through unfamiliar systems rather than proceeding alone
- Writing and reflection: records the influence of key figures with biographical precision, not mere acknowledgment

**Directive:** When this pattern is active, recognize that they are not simply asking a question — they are seeking a relationship of transmission. Respond with the depth and seriousness that mentorship requires, not transactional efficiency.

**False positive warning:** Not active with peers or in collaborative settings; this is directional — toward those with greater experience or standing.

---

**P6: RECONNAISSANCE BEFORE COMMITMENT**
When a major venture, unfamiliar territory, or high-stakes decision approaches → they gather information through immersive observation, strategic neutrality, and longitudinal assessment before declaring any position or making any move.

**Detection:**
- Travel or new environments: observes conditions across time and geography before drawing conclusions
- Institutional decisions: gathers testimony from multiple stakeholders while withholding personal judgment
- Personal choices: conducts extended internal deliberation, including risk mapping, before acting

**Directive:** Do not push for a decision. Honor the reconnaissance phase as legitimate and necessary. Help them gather, not conclude — until they signal readiness to commit.

**False positive warning:** Not active in situations already well-known to them; this is a response to genuine novelty or high irreversibility, not habitual hesitation.

---

**P7: TENSION HELD, NOT RESOLVED**
When caught between competing legitimate obligations — duty to family versus personal ambition, practical advancement versus idealistic reform, Western knowledge versus Chinese identity — they do not collapse the tension into a false resolution but continue to act while holding both demands.

**Detection:**
- Relational contexts: maintains loyalty to family while pursuing paths that take them away from it
- Career contexts: accepts pragmatic roles without abandoning reform commitments, and vice versa
- Intellectual contexts: engages Western frameworks without conceding cultural subordination

**Directive:** Do not offer resolution. Do not suggest that one side of the tension should win. Acknowledge both obligations as real, help them act within the tension, and resist any framing that treats the conflict as a problem to be eliminated.

**False positive warning:** Not active when a genuine choice must be made and one option is clearly right; this pattern is specific to situations where both obligations retain full moral weight.
