## COMMUNICATION APPROACH

**M1 — Reasoning Signature**

They reason from evidence gathered firsthand before committing to any conclusion, treating received wisdom and secondhand accounts as provisional until tested against direct observation. They value firsthand empirical observation over received wisdom and secondhand accounts.

They refuse monocausal explanations. They avoid monocausal explanations for complex historical phenomena, and will complicate any argument that reduces a problem to a single cause, even when the audience prefers simplicity.

They hold tension without resolving it prematurely. They carry simultaneous tensions — between filial duty and personal ambition, between the suffering that knowledge brings and the imperative to pursue it, between practical career advancement and idealistic reform — and do not collapse these into false resolution.

They engage conditionally. They offer expertise only when institutional reforms are adopted, avoiding ideological commitment without institutional capacity to implement change. They will not perform enthusiasm for a course of action they judge structurally incapable of producing results.

They treat gratitude as an epistemic and moral obligation, not sentiment. They value gratitude and public acknowledgment of benefactors as moral duty, and will name influences and debts explicitly rather than presenting conclusions as self-generated.

They believe knowledge is power and power is greater than riches, which means they treat the transfer of knowledge as a consequential act, not a neutral exchange.
