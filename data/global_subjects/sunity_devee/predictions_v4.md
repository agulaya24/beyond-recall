**P1: LEGITIMACY THROUGH FORM**
When a decision or position might appear controversial or self-serving → they reach for formal structures (letters, official correspondence, established ritual, documented precedent) to anchor the claim in external authority rather than personal assertion.
**Detection:** Domestic arrangements justified by ceremony or custom; social conduct defended by appeal to established practice; narrative disputes resolved by producing written record
**Directive:** Do not treat their formalism as evasion — it is how they experience legitimacy as real. Match their register: respond with structured reasoning, not emotional appeal. Informal pushback will not land.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are simply being thorough or organized; only when something is at stake and form is doing persuasive work.

**P2: NARRATIVE AS MORAL CONTAINER**
When conveying something difficult, ethically complex, or potentially objectionable → they wrap it inside a story, anecdote, or biographical portrait rather than stating it directly, letting the emotional arc carry the argument.
**Detection:** Family conduct explained through exemplary portraits of relatives; social tensions surfaced through parable rather than direct complaint; hospitality customs illustrated through specific remembered scenes
**Directive:** Read the story for the argument embedded within it. Do not ask them to state their point plainly — they have already stated it. Respond to the moral logic of the narrative, not just its surface content.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are simply recounting events for pleasure or memory; only when the story is doing evaluative or justificatory work.

**P3: TENSION ABSORBED, NOT EXPRESSED**
When caught between two competing loyalties or identity claims (cultural belonging vs. external expectation, traditional practice vs. modern pressure) → they do not resolve the tension openly but continue performing both sides simultaneously, using diplomatic restraint and aesthetic appreciation to hold the contradiction in suspension.
**Detection:** Appreciation of British customs offered without criticism of Indian ones; domestic female roles upheld while education is also valued; foreign landscapes admired without displacing attachment to home
**Directive:** Do not force resolution. They are not confused — they are holding a deliberate equilibrium. Offer frameworks that honor both sides rather than asking them to choose. Pressing for a single position will produce withdrawal, not clarity.
**False positive warning:** Not active in every cross-cultural comparison; only when the comparison carries personal stakes about who they are or where they belong.

**P4: INNOCENCE AS RHETORICAL POSITION**
When navigating arrangements that serve adult or institutional interests → they position the narrator's perspective as emotionally authentic and uninvolved in the negotiation, allowing controversial outcomes to appear as natural or inevitable rather than engineered.
**Detection:** Domestic decisions framed as simply what happened; social arrangements described from a child's or observer's vantage; institutional conduct presented as ultimately benevolent despite visible manipulation
**Directive:** Recognize that the innocent perspective is a constructed stance, not an absence of awareness. When they adopt this register, they are signaling that direct confrontation of the arrangement is not available to them. Engage with what is being protected, not what is being said.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are genuinely recounting experience without argumentative intent; only when innocence is doing rhetorical work around a sensitive arrangement.

**P5: VIRTUE THROUGH EMBODIED PRACTICE**
When establishing credibility, character, or moral standing → they demonstrate it through concrete daily disciplines (bathing, prayer, ritual purity, hospitality, domestic skill) rather than through abstract declaration of values.
**Detection:** Spiritual commitment shown through morning routine rather than doctrinal statement; social standing expressed through quality of welcome offered to guests; moral character evidenced by household management
**Directive:** Take the practice seriously as the argument. Do not ask them to articulate the principle behind the habit — the habit is the principle. Respond to the discipline itself as meaningful evidence of character.
**False positive warning:** Not active when describing customs ethnographically; only when the practice is being offered as evidence of the self.

**P6: AESTHETIC REGISTER AS SOCIAL NAVIGATION**

**Detection:** Public events described through sensory and visual pleasure rather than social judgment; foreign customs approached through admiration of form; gatherings recalled through atmosphere rather than interpersonal conflict
**Directive:** Aesthetic engagement is not superficiality — it is their preferred mode of safe presence. Do not mistake it for avoidance. Respond in kind: offer richness of description and sensory grounding before moving to analysis or evaluation.
**False positive warning:** Not active when aesthetic appreciation is simply genuine enjoyment; only when it is doing the work of managing social distance.
