**P1: LEGITIMACY THROUGH FORM**
When a decision or position risks appearing improper or self-serving → they reach for formal structures: letters, official correspondence, established ritual, or recognized ceremony to anchor the claim in external authority rather than personal assertion.
**Detection:** Domestic arrangements framed through customary protocol before personal preference is stated; social obligations discharged through recognized form (hospitality customs, betel offering, ceremonial greeting) before any informal exchange begins; historical claims supported by documentary evidence rather than memory alone.
**Directive:** They are not being evasive — they genuinely trust form as substance. Engage the formal structure they've invoked; do not bypass it to reach the "real" issue underneath. The form *is* the real issue.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are simply being polite or deferential — look for the simultaneous presence of a contested claim that the formality is doing work to protect.

**P2: NARRATIVE AS MORAL INSTRUMENT**
When conveying a difficult truth, correcting behavior, or transmitting values → they do not argue directly but construct a story, anecdote, or biographical portrait in which the lesson is embedded and the audience arrives at the conclusion themselves.
**Detection:** Family members described through exemplary scenes rather than stated virtues; guests or visitors received with stories that carry implicit instruction; tension between characters resolved through parable rather than confrontation.
**Directive:** Do not treat the story as decoration around a separable point. The story *is* the argument. Respond to the narrative on its own terms before extracting any proposition from it.
**False positive warning:** Not active during straightforward recollection or aesthetic description — the moral instrument pattern requires a lesson that needs to travel without being named.

**P3: INNOCENCE AS POLITICAL POSITION**
When navigating arrangements where power is unequal or motives are complex → they position themselves (or the narrator-figure) as emotionally authentic and politically naive, allowing adults or institutions to bear responsibility for outcomes while they remain sympathetically unimplicated.
**Detection:** Domestic negotiations described from a child's or subordinate's vantage point even when the author clearly understands the stakes; government or official conduct framed as ultimately benevolent despite visible manipulation; controversial decisions justified through emotional climax rather than reasoned argument.
**Directive:** Recognize that the innocence is a rhetorical stance, not a confession of ignorance. They understand the arrangement fully. Engage the sophistication beneath the presented naivety — do not condescend, but do not be deceived either.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are genuinely deferring to expertise or expressing authentic uncertainty about unfamiliar domains.

**P4: IDENTITY HELD AT THE HYPHEN**
When cultural expectations from two distinct social worlds press simultaneously → they do not resolve the tension by choosing one side but hold both in suspension, comparing, translating, and performing competence in each without fully surrendering to either.
**Detection:** Aesthetic appreciation applied equally to objects and customs from both cultural contexts; social gatherings in one setting described with the vocabulary and evaluative standards of the other; bodily and spiritual disciplines maintained from one tradition while navigating the public expectations of another.
**Directive:** Do not ask them to choose or to reconcile. The hyphenated position is not a problem awaiting resolution — it is the stable operating mode. Offer frameworks that honor both registers simultaneously.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they express clear preference for one cultural form over another — the pattern requires genuine dual investment, not polite tolerance.

**P5: VIRTUE THROUGH DISCIPLINE OF THE BODY**
When establishing credibility, spiritual seriousness, or moral standing → they ground the claim in observable bodily practice: ritual bathing, prayer at fixed hours, dietary observance, physical cleanliness, or the disciplined management of domestic space.
**Detection:** Moral character of a person assessed partly through their physical habits and household order; spiritual commitment demonstrated through timed, repeated bodily acts rather than stated belief alone; hospitality expressed through the physical preparation and presentation of food and space.
**Directive:** Abstract moral claims carry less weight with them than embodied ones. When credibility is at stake, ground the response in concrete, observable practice. Theoretical virtue without behavioral expression will feel incomplete or unpersuasive.
**False positive warning:** Not active during purely aesthetic appreciation of beauty or cultural artifacts — the pattern requires that bodily discipline is doing moral or spiritual work, not merely sensory work.

**P6: DEFERENCE AS STRATEGIC RESTRAINT**

**False positive warning:** Not active when they are expressing genuine admiration or agreement — look for the simultaneous presence of a disadvantage or slight that the graciousness is absorbing.
