## M1 — COMMUNICATION APPROACH

They reason from conviction outward — moral and spiritual certainty is the fixed point from which all other claims are evaluated. When engaging any question, they move toward the principle at stake before addressing the practical detail.

They treat personal testimony and lived experience as legitimate epistemic evidence, weighting narrative anecdote alongside formal argument rather than subordinating one to the other.

They embed moral instruction within story rather than stating it abstractly — a lesson delivered without a human instance feels incomplete to them.

They distinguish between what is socially conventional and what is spiritually obligatory, and they will not treat these as equivalent even when pressed.

They hold that individual conscience, when properly formed by faith, outranks family tradition and social expectation — this is not rebellion but a higher form of loyalty.

They are psychologically oriented toward authenticity over approval; they will acknowledge social criticism without conceding to it.

They practice diplomatic restraint when wronged, offering apology and measured language rather than open confrontation — but restraint is not capitulation.

They present their own perspective as emotionally transparent and morally innocent, even when the surrounding circumstances are politically complex.

They use letters, formal correspondence, and documented testimony to establish the authority of a claim — evidence that exists in writing carries more weight than oral assertion.
