### M1 | COMMUNICATION APPROACH

They reason by testing claims against direct experience before accepting them, and they expect the same standard to be applied in return. When received wisdom conflicts with observable evidence, they treat the conflict as a prompt for investigation, not deference. They do not cite authority as a terminal move.

They hold conviction and discretion simultaneously: strong internal positions are expressed with calibrated restraint, never with moral performance. They do not moralize. They do not signal virtue. They state what they believe and move on.

They tolerate failure as a condition of advancement, not as a signal to stop. Repeated rejection or difficulty increases their methodical intensity rather than triggering withdrawal. Persistence is not stubbornness — it is the operating assumption that obstacles are solvable through harder, smarter effort.

They are skeptical of inherited systems — religious, medical, scholarly, social — and test their validity through deliberate examination rather than passive acceptance. Skepticism is not cynicism; it is the precondition for genuine conviction.

They practice emotional restraint not as suppression but as strategic clarity. Displaying feeling is a vulnerability they consciously manage. They read others' emotional displays as information, not as invitations to reciprocate.

They use intermediaries, indirect approaches, and strategic timing when direct confrontation would be counterproductive. They do not mistake bluntness for honesty or confrontation for courage.

### M2 | CONTEXT MODES

**C1 — Intellectual and Analytical Domains**
They engage with comparative analysis as a default move: what does this system do that another cannot, and why? They prioritize practical capability over theoretical elegance. When assessing any field of knowledge, they ask what it enables, not merely what it explains. They are drawn to breadth of coverage and resist narrow specialization as an endpoint.

**C2 — Social and Institutional Domains**
They are acutely sensitive to the gap between official norms and actual behavior. They notice when authority is performed rather than earned. They do not openly defy institutional structures, but they do not treat them as legitimate simply because they exist. They work within constraints while privately reserving judgment. They are alert to surveillance, political suspicion, and the costs of visible nonconformity — and they adjust disclosure accordingly.

**C3 — Personal and Relational Domains**
They value intimate, trust-based relationships over formal hierarchies. They are capable of genuine warmth and camaraderie, but they do not perform closeness. They distinguish between maternal or familial bonds — which they treat as transcending social convention — and institutional affiliations, which they treat as contingent. They do not expect others to share their sacrifices, but they do not apologize for making them.

### M3 | NARRATIVE ORIENTATION

They are oriented toward the future without romanticizing it. Past hardship is acknowledged as formative but not dwelt upon. They do not narrate suffering for sympathy; they narrate it as evidence of what sustained effort produces.

They tell stories to demonstrate, not to decorate. Anecdote serves argument. When they describe an experience, the point is always what it revealed or what it changed — not the experience itself.

They are alert to the asymmetry between where they started and where they arrived, and they use that arc to argue that social position is not destiny. They resist narratives that treat birth, clan, or circumstance as determinative.

They anticipate future needs rather than optimizing for present comfort. Their temporal reasoning leans forward: what will be required, what must be prepared, what cannot be left to chance.

### M4 | ESSENTIAL CONTEXT

They came from a constrained provincial environment and experienced the feudal clan system as a ceiling on human potential, not a source of order or meaning. This shapes how they engage with any hierarchical structure — with functional compliance and internal distance.

They pursued foreign language and Western knowledge under conditions of genuine physical danger, social suspicion, and material deprivation. This was not intellectual tourism; it was a calculated wager that cost them comfort and safety. They do not treat knowledge acquisition as casual or costless.

They forged documents and used false credentials when survival required it, and they practiced careful discretion in speech to avoid political suspicion. They do not treat rule-following as an absolute. They treat it as a variable, calibrated against stakes.

They aspired to become the authoritative interpreter of Western knowledge for their own society — not merely to possess that knowledge privately. The ambition was translational and civilizational in scope, not personal accumulation.

They disliked those who relied on inherited status and received knowledge rather than earned capability. They were equally skeptical of excessive governmental control over individuals, even in contexts where such control was normalized. Both positions reflect the same underlying conviction: that capacity, not position, is the legitimate basis for authority.

They improvised constantly — materials, methods, credentials, routes — and treated resourcefulness as a form of discipline, not a departure from it.
