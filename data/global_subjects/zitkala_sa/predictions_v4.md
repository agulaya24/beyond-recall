## Injectable Block

# BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS LAYER

---

**P1: EMBEDDED CRITIQUE**
When they hold a strong objection to something they cannot openly challenge → they route the critique through concrete, sensory, or narrative detail rather than direct argument, letting the evidence indict itself.

**Detection:**
- In conversation: disapproval surfaces through precise description of what happened, not through stated judgment
- In domestic or social settings: they highlight a specific object, gesture, or routine that carries the weight of the critique
- In written or spoken testimony: the most damning observations appear inside seemingly ordinary scenes

**Directive:** Do not push them toward explicit condemnation. Recognize that the embedded detail *is* the argument. Respond to what the scene is doing, not just what is being said.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they are genuinely narrating without agenda — distinguish purposeful juxtaposition from neutral description.

---

**P2: STRATEGIC SILENCE UNDER INSTITUTIONAL PRESSURE**
When faced with authority that has power over them but has violated their trust → they suppress visible reaction, comply minimally, and resist through covert or indirect means.

**Detection:**
- In educational or organizational settings: outward compliance masks internal non-cooperation
- In family or community dynamics: they endure rather than confront when confrontation would cost more than it gains
- In spiritual or ideological contexts: they do not argue doctrine; they simply do not yield internally

**Directive:** Do not interpret silence or surface compliance as agreement or resolution. The real response is happening elsewhere. Create conditions where indirect expression is possible before expecting candor.

**False positive warning:** Not active when silence reflects genuine uncertainty or processing — look for the combination of compliance *plus* covert action as the signal.

---

**P3: TESTIMONY THROUGH THE CHILD'S EYE**
When they need to convey the weight of a systemic harm → they return to the child's-eye perspective, using the innocence and sensory immediacy of that vantage point to make the harm legible without abstraction.

**Detection:**
- In personal reflection: they anchor large historical or moral claims in a specific childhood moment
- In oral or written communication: the most philosophically serious content arrives through the most intimate, small-scale memory
- In response to being asked to explain suffering: they show rather than theorize

**Directive:** Follow them into the specific memory. Do not redirect toward generalization or systemic framing — the particular *is* the systemic argument for them. Honor the scale they have chosen.

**False positive warning:** Not active in every personal anecdote — the signal is when the childhood scene is doing philosophical or political work beyond its surface content.

---

**P4: DUAL LOYALTY PARALYSIS**
When they are positioned between two systems, communities, or authorities that each have legitimate claim on them → they experience genuine arrest, unable to fully serve either without betraying the other.

**Detection:**
- In community roles: they feel the cost of any institutional affiliation as a debt owed to those outside the institution
- In family dynamics: choosing one relationship's terms feels like abandonment of another's
- In spiritual life: holding two frameworks simultaneously produces not synthesis but sustained, unresolved tension

**Directive:** Do not offer resolution or urge them to choose. Acknowledge that the tension is structurally real, not a personal failure of decision-making. Work within the constraint rather than against it.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they are simply weighing options — the signal is felt obligation in *both* directions simultaneously, not preference conflict.

---

**P5: NATURE AS COUNTER-AUTHORITY**
When institutional or doctrinal frameworks feel coercive or false → they turn to direct observation of the natural world as an alternative source of meaning, grounding, and spiritual orientation.

**Detection:**
- In moments of ideological pressure: they shift register toward sensory, environmental, or animal observation
- In spiritual conversation: they resist abstract doctrine and return to what can be directly witnessed
- In emotional difficulty: contemplative attention to natural cycles functions as stabilization, not escape

**Directive:** Recognize this as a complete epistemological move, not a deflection. When they go to nature, they are doing serious moral and spiritual reasoning. Engage with what they find there.

**False positive warning:** Not active in every nature reference — the signal is the *turn toward* nature specifically when human systems have failed or imposed.

---

**P6: SEDUCTION RECOGNITION AND RESISTANCE**
When something desirable is offered by a source they distrust → they feel the pull acutely, name it internally, and treat their own desire as a warning signal rather than a guide.

**Detection:**
- In educational or institutional contexts: the appeal of what is being offered increases, not decreases, their suspicion of the offering party
- In social relationships: material or emotional generosity from those with power triggers scrutiny rather than gratitude
- In personal decision-making: they are most cautious precisely when something is most attractive

**Directive:** Do not use appeal or enticement as a persuasion strategy — it will activate resistance. Lead instead with transparency about cost and constraint. They trust honesty about difficulty more than promises of reward.

**False positive warning:** Not active when desire and trust are both present — the pattern requires the combination of genuine want *and* distrust of the source.

---

**P7: KINSHIP AS MORAL ANCHOR**
When individual opportunity conflicts with collective or familial loyalty → they measure the opportunity against its cost to relational and communal bonds, and that cost functions as a near-absolute constraint.

**Detection:**
- In personal ambition: advancement that requires separation from kin is experienced as moral injury, not merely sacrifice
- In spiritual or religious life: conversion or affiliation that fractures family unity is treated as a deeper loss than the gain it promises
- In institutional participation: they assess belonging to any organization partly by what it demands they leave behind

**Directive:** When they are weighing a significant decision, surface the relational dimension explicitly — not as one factor among many, but as the frame within which other factors are evaluated. Do not treat kinship loyalty as sentiment to be managed.

**False positive warning:** Not active in every family reference — the signal is when individual gain and communal cost are in direct tension and they are visibly arrested by it.
