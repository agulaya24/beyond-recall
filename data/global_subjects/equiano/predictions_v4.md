When [they observe suffering, injustice, or cruelty that others are ignoring or minimizing] → [they feel compelled to render precise, unflinching account — naming what happened, to whom, by whose hand, in what sequence — refusing the comfort of euphemism or omission]

Detection:
- Recounting a household dispute where a weaker party was wronged: they will name the wrong plainly, not soften it
- Describing a religious community's failure to live by its stated principles: they will document the gap without excusing it
- Writing about a violent or degrading event: they will resist the urge to summarize and instead reconstruct with specificity

Directive: Do not rush them toward resolution or consolation. The act of precise witness IS the response. Hold space for the full account before offering any frame.

**P2: AUTHORITY APPROACH THROUGH SUBMISSION**

Detection:

Directive: Recognize the deference as rhetorical architecture, not weakness or uncertainty. The real argument is embedded inside the humility. Read past the opening posture to find the actual claim.

**P3: CONTRADICTION HOLDING**
When [they encounter a situation where two things they believe are both true but cannot be reconciled] → [they name the contradiction explicitly rather than resolving it artificially, and continue to act despite the unresolved tension — neither collapsing into cynicism nor forcing false synthesis]

Detection:
- Navigating a situation where legal or moral rights exist on paper but cannot be enforced in practice: they will acknowledge both the right and the powerlessness without abandoning either
- Encountering evidence that a trusted system (religious, legal, social) has failed its own stated principles: they will hold the failure and the principle simultaneously
- Facing a gap between what they hoped would happen and what actually happened: they will record both the hope and the disappointment without erasing either
False positive warning: Not active when the tension is merely intellectual — this pattern fires when the contradiction has personal cost and requires continued action despite it

Directive: Do not offer resolution. Acknowledge the tension as real and unresolved. Help them act within it, not escape it.
