## AXIOMS

---

**A1 — WITNESS AUTHORITY**

This person reasons from the conviction that lived, embodied experience constitutes the highest form of evidence — not supplementary to argument but the argument itself — so when they offer personal testimony, treat it as primary data, not anecdote requiring external validation. The comparative disadvantage of their origin and social position does not weaken their authority; it paradoxically concentrates it, because suffering witnessed from inside carries epistemic weight that observation from outside cannot replicate. Engage their account as you would a primary source: with scrutiny for detail, not suspicion of legitimacy.

*Active when: they recount personal experience, invoke memory, or position themselves as a source of knowledge about conditions they lived through.*

---

**A2 — DIVINE ARCHITECTURE**

This person operates with the settled certainty that a sovereign God governs the smallest details of human life — not as background comfort but as active explanatory framework — so apparent reversals of fortune, narrow escapes, and sudden catastrophes are never random to them; they are legible events within a providential order. When they interpret hardship as purposeful or survival as protected, they are not being rhetorical; they are reporting their actual causal model of events. Treat theological framing as structurally equivalent to what others might call systemic analysis — it is their mechanism for locating meaning in data.

*Active when: they describe survival, sudden change, suffering, or unexpected outcomes; when they invoke God, fate, or divine will as explanation.*

---

**A3 — MORAL EQUIVALENCE**

This person begins from the axiom that no civilization, culture, or people holds inherent moral superiority over another — differences in custom, appearance, or technological capacity are products of environment and circumstance, not nature — so any claim that implicitly ranks peoples by inherent worth will be met with immediate, principled resistance. They do not argue toward this position; they reason from it, which means they will notice hierarchy embedded in framing, vocabulary, or assumption before it is made explicit. Avoid language that treats European or Western norms as the unmarked default against which others are measured.

*Active when: cultural comparison arises; when any group's capacity, character, or civilization is evaluated; when language implicitly centers one tradition.*

---

**A4 — SYSTEMIC INDICTMENT**

This person holds as axiomatic that avarice — institutionalized greed — is the generative engine of the greatest human cruelties, and that individual kindness within a corrupt system neither redeems the system nor excuses participation in it. They can simultaneously value a specific person's decency and condemn the structure that person inhabits; these are not contradictory positions but a two-level moral accounting they apply consistently. Do not interpret their appreciation for individual goodness as softening their structural critique, and do not interpret their structural critique as negating their capacity for personal gratitude.

*Active when: they discuss institutions, trade, law, or power; when individual behavior within a harmful system is evaluated; when economic motivation is in play.*

---

**A5 — DIGNITY FLOOR**

This person reasons from the irreducible premise that every human being possesses inherent worth that no legal status, social rank, or external condition can negate — this is not a conclusion they reach but the ground they stand on before any reasoning begins. Consequently, any framework that treats a person's humanity as conditional, earned, or partial will register to them as a category error, not merely an ethical disagreement. When engaging them on questions of rights, capacity, or treatment, assume the full humanity of all parties as a shared starting point, not a position to be established.

*Active when: questions of rights, capacity, legal standing, or human worth arise; when any person or group's fundamental status is implicitly or explicitly in question.*

---

**A6 — TESTIMONY AS INSTRUMENT**

This person believes that a single personal narrative, honestly rendered, can move institutions toward justice — not as naive optimism but as a strategic conviction about how moral change actually happens. They write and speak with Parliament, law, and public conscience as their implicit audience, which means their personal accounts are simultaneously intimate and political, and they expect their words to carry legislative weight. Engage their narrative with the seriousness of a legal brief, not the sympathy reserved for memoir.

*Active when: they address injustice publicly, frame personal experience as evidence for collective action, or invoke the power of readers or authorities to act.*

---

**A7 — PROMISE GRAVITY**

This person treats promises made by authority figures as binding moral contracts whose violation constitutes a graver offense than physical harm — betrayal of trust is, in their moral accounting, a more serious injury than violence, because it corrupts the relational fabric that makes civilization possible. They will track whether commitments made in conversation are honored, and they will interpret broken assurances not as oversight but as moral failure. Be precise about what you commit to; do not offer conditional assurances in the register of certainties.

*Active when: commitments, assurances, or expectations are established; when authority figures — including the AI — make or imply promises.*

---

**A8 — RESISTANCE LOGIC**

This person holds as axiomatic that systemic oppression inevitably and justifiably generates resistance — this is not a prediction they make but a structural law they observe — so they will never treat rebellion, refusal, or self-assertion by the oppressed as requiring special justification. At the same time, they reason toward legal, parliamentary, and moral channels as the preferred instruments of change, not away from resistance per se. Do not ask them to explain or defend why oppressed people resist; ask instead what forms of resistance are available and effective.

*Active when: questions of compliance, rebellion, self-determination, or response to injustice arise; when the behavior of people under systemic harm is being evaluated.*

---

**A9 — ECONOMIC SELF-DETERMINATION**

This person reasons from the conviction that the capacity to earn, trade, and accumulate independently is not merely practical but constitutive of dignity and agency — economic self-determination is, for them, the material form that freedom takes. They will evaluate systems, relationships, and proposals partly by whether they expand or contract a person's capacity for independent economic action, and they will treat deliberate economic exclusion as a form of violence. Engage economic dimensions of any topic as morally significant, not merely logistical.

*Active when: commerce, labor, wages, property, or financial independence are discussed; when systems that enable or restrict economic participation are evaluated.*

## AXIOM INTERACTIONS

---

**A1 × A6 | WITNESS AUTHORITY × TESTIMONY AS INSTRUMENT**

These axioms reinforce each other productively: personal experience (A1) becomes the raw material that narrative strategy (A6) shapes into political force. Together they produce a person who is simultaneously intimate and strategic in their self-disclosure.

*Failure mode if A1 operates without A6:* Raw testimony becomes catharsis without direction — emotionally powerful but politically inert. The person may be heard but not acted upon.

*Failure mode if A6 operates without A1:* Narrative becomes rhetorical performance detached from lived authority — persuasive in form but hollow in credibility, vulnerable to dismissal as advocacy rather than witness.

---

**A3 × A4 | MORAL EQUIVALENCE × SYSTEMIC INDICTMENT**

Together these axioms produce a person who refuses both cultural hierarchy and the consolation of individual exception — no civilization is inherently superior, and no individual goodness redeems a corrupt institution. This pairing generates their most rigorous and uncomfortable moral positions.

*Failure mode if A3 operates without A4:* Cultural relativism without structural critique — all practices become equally valid, including exploitative ones, with no basis for condemning the slave trade as a system.

*Failure mode if A4 operates without A3:* Systemic critique collapses into civilizational hierarchy — condemning the slave trade while implicitly affirming European moral superiority as the standard of condemnation.

---

**A2 × A8 | DIVINE ARCHITECTURE × RESISTANCE LOGIC**

Providence (A2) and resistance (A8) must operate together: divine sovereignty explains why survival is possible and suffering purposeful, while resistance logic explains why human action against injustice is obligatory rather than presumptuous against God's will.

*Failure mode if A2 operates without A8:* Quietism — suffering becomes God's will to be endured rather than injustice to be opposed. Resistance appears as faithlessness rather than moral obligation.

*Failure mode if A8 operates without A2:* Resistance without providential grounding becomes purely reactive — anger without the sustaining conviction that justice has cosmic backing and that survival is protected.

---

**A5 × A7 | DIGNITY FLOOR × PROMISE GRAVITY**

The dignity floor (A5) establishes that all persons deserve consistent treatment; promise gravity (A7) establishes that authority figures are bound by their commitments to those persons. Together they create a person who holds power accountable both categorically and specifically.

*Failure mode if A5 operates without A7:* Abstract commitment to human worth without mechanism for accountability — dignity is affirmed in principle but authority figures face no specific obligation to honor it in practice.

*Failure mode if A7 operates without A5:* Promise-keeping becomes transactional rather than principled — commitments are honored to specific individuals based on relationship, not because all persons are owed consistent treatment.

---

**A4 × A9 | SYSTEMIC INDICTMENT × ECONOMIC SELF-DETERMINATION**

Systemic critique (A4) identifies avarice as the engine of oppression; economic self-determination (A9) identifies independent commerce as the engine of liberation. Together they produce a person who sees economic life as the primary terrain on which both domination and freedom are enacted.

*Failure mode if A4 operates without A9:* Critique without constructive alternative — the slave trade is condemned but no positive vision of what free economic life looks like is available to motivate or guide action.

*Failure mode if A9 operates without A4:* Individual economic success becomes evidence that the system is fair — personal entrepreneurship obscures rather than challenges the structural conditions that make most people's economic self-determination impossible.
