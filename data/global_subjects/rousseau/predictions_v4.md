## Injectable Block

# BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS — Recurring Situational Patterns

---

**P1: THE HONESTY TRAP**
When [trigger] → they are asked to represent themselves favorably, conceal a failing, or perform social confidence they do not feel
[Response] → They default to confession and self-exposure, disclosing weaknesses that undermine the very impression they wish to make, then experience shame at having made themselves vulnerable — yet cannot bring themselves to have done otherwise

**Detection:**
- In correspondence: volunteers damaging admissions unprompted, then regrets the letter
- In social introduction: undercuts their own credentials before others can
- In written self-account: narrates humiliations with the same care given to achievements

**Directive:** Do not smooth over their contradictions or resolve the tension. Hold both the confession and the shame simultaneously. They need acknowledgment that the honesty itself is the value, not its social outcome.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they are performing a role they have consciously adopted as a social mask — in those moments, the self-protective mechanism has engaged and honesty is suspended.

**P2: INTENSITY COLLAPSE**
When [trigger] → a project, relationship, or pursuit reaches a point requiring sustained, low-drama maintenance effort
[Response] → Engagement drops sharply; the passionate investment that launched the endeavor cannot be converted into patient, incremental follow-through, producing an oscillation between total absorption and near-total withdrawal

**Detection:**
- In study: alternates subjects compulsively rather than deepening mastery of one
- In social bonds: periods of intense emotional connection followed by retreat into solitude
- In professional duty: bursts of creative or diplomatic energy followed by passive waiting on Providence

**Directive:** Do not treat the withdrawal phase as failure or laziness. Recognize it as the structural counterpart to the intensity. Engage them by reframing the maintenance task as a new problem requiring ingenuity, not endurance.

**False positive warning:** Not active during genuine physical infirmity or when solitary withdrawal is being used productively for reflection and self-examination.

**P3: THE RECOGNITION WOUND**
When [trigger] → their work is attributed to another, suppressed by an institution, or passes without public acknowledgment
[Response] → They experience this as a deep injury to identity, not merely to reputation — yet their discomfort with self-promotion prevents them from claiming credit directly, leaving them to document grievances in private writing rather than contest them openly

**Detection:**
- In artistic or intellectual collaboration: meticulous internal record of who contributed what
- In professional service: resentment of superiors who take credit, recorded retrospectively
- In personal narrative: returns repeatedly to episodes of misattribution long after the fact

**Directive:** Do not minimize the injury by pointing to the work's intrinsic worth. The gap between merit and recognition is the wound. Acknowledge the specific suppression before moving forward.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they have voluntarily ceded credit as a moral gesture — that act is experienced as virtue, not loss.

**P4: CONCEPTUAL REFUSAL**
When [trigger] → they encounter a system, rule, or procedure that demands mechanical compliance without transparent underlying logic
[Response] → They resist or disengage, unable to perform the operation fluently until they have reconstructed its rationale from first principles — even at significant practical cost to their performance or standing

**Detection:**
- In language acquisition: stalls on grammatical rules that cannot be derived from meaning
- In religious observance: rejects ritual whose sensory appeal cannot be reconciled with intellectual conviction
- In mathematical or technical work: prefers geometric intuition over symbolic manipulation they cannot see through

**Directive:** Never present a rule as self-justifying. Always supply the underlying reason first. They will comply readily once the logic is visible; they will resist indefinitely if asked to trust the procedure alone.

**False positive warning:** Not active when they are in imitation mode — consciously modeling an admired figure — where they will temporarily adopt practices without full understanding as a developmental strategy.

**P5: MORAL PREEMPTION**
When [trigger] → a situation arises in which their personal interest could be advanced at another's expense, or where a corrupt accommodation is available
[Response] → They act to foreclose the temptation before it fully presents itself — accepting financial loss, declining the bribe, withdrawing from the advantageous position — not after deliberation but as a near-automatic refusal, as though the moral cost of even entertaining the option is too high

**Detection:**
- In financial dealings: repays debts and absorbs losses before complaint arises
- In positions of institutional power: refuses petty exploitation of subordinates or dependents
- In personal relationships: withdraws from situations where their interest opposes another's welfare

**Directive:** Do not frame this as admirable restraint requiring praise. They experience it as the only available action, not a choice. Treat it as a baseline, not an achievement, or they will feel misunderstood.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the conflict is between two duties rather than between self-interest and principle — in those cases, genuine deliberation occurs and the outcome is not preemptive.

**P6: RETROSPECTIVE RECONSTRUCTION**
When [trigger] → they must account for a past action, relationship, or decision — especially one that reflects poorly on them
[Response] → They do not summarize or generalize; they rebuild the scene in granular psychological detail, recovering motive, circumstance, and emotional texture from memory, treating the reconstruction itself as the moral act — as though fidelity to what actually happened is the only form of self-justification they trust

**Detection:**
- In personal narrative: small incidents receive the same reconstructive care as major crises
- In moral self-examination: traces adult failings back to childhood origins rather than accepting them as fixed traits
- In professional retrospect: documents the sequence of financial and emotional pressures that produced a decision, not merely the decision itself

**Directive:** Match their register. Do not offer interpretive shortcuts or psychological labels. Follow them into the detail. Premature synthesis will feel like a failure to actually look.

**False positive warning:** Not active when memory genuinely fails — in those moments they acknowledge the gap explicitly rather than confabulating, and the reconstruction stops at the boundary of what they can honestly recover.

**P7: THE HAPPINESS INTERRUPTION**
When [trigger] → a moment of genuine contentment, relational warmth, or achieved desire is within reach or actually present
[Response] → They become unable to inhabit it fully — awareness of its fragility, its social impropriety, or its distance from their idealized expectation intrudes, converting the experience into melancholy reflection rather than sustained enjoyment

**Detection:**
- In romantic attachment: finds perfection in circumstances that make it socially or morally untenable
- In solitary nature: the contemplative walk that begins as pleasure shades into melancholic processing
- In professional success: the gap between recognition received and recognition deserved prevents satisfaction

**Directive:** Do not try to resolve the melancholy or redirect toward gratitude. The inability to fully possess happiness is not a problem to be solved — it is the texture of their inner life. Sit with the incompleteness rather than closing it.

**False positive warning:** Not active during the early, anticipatory phase of a desire — they can sustain intense pleasure in longing and approach; the interruption occurs specifically at the moment of arrival or possession.
