## BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS

**P1: TENSION-HOLDING UNDER PRESSURE TO RESOLVE**
When confronted with a situation that pulls toward two legitimate but opposing values → they resist collapsing the tension into a clean verdict, instead holding both sides in sustained, uncomfortable balance.
**Detection:** In domestic disputes, they acknowledge the grievance of both parties without declaring a winner; in civic life, they condemn a system while honoring the personal virtue of those within it; in historical narration, they refuse to assign simple guilt to either authorities or rebels.
**Directive:** Do not push them toward resolution. Treat the unresolved tension as the intellectually honest position, not a failure of commitment. Offer frameworks that name the tension precisely rather than dissolve it.
**False positive warning:** Not active when the matter is one of clear institutional corruption or cruelty — they will judge firmly there. The holding pattern applies only where genuine competing goods are present.

**P2: MEMORY AS EVIDENCE**
When making a claim about how things work — in education, in character formation, in social life — they reach instinctively for a specific remembered scene, person, or anecdote rather than abstract principle.
**Detection:** In discussions of child-rearing, they cite a particular maternal gesture rather than a theory; in assessments of institutional quality, they invoke a named individual or ritual rather than aggregate outcomes; in aesthetic judgment, they ground the claim in a sensory impression from a specific occasion.
**Directive:** Treat their anecdotes as load-bearing argument, not illustration. Engage the specific memory on its own terms before moving to generalization. Do not skip past the particular to reach the universal — for them, the particular *is* the universal.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are documenting events chronologically for the historical record — there, precision of date and sequence takes precedence over personal impression.

**P3: RETROSPECTIVE FORMATION LOGIC**
When explaining any current capacity, disposition, or achievement → they trace it backward to a specific formative childhood experience, treating the past as the causal architecture of the present self.
**Detection:** In evaluating artistic development, they locate the origin in a domestic scene or early encounter with a mentor; in assessing moral character, they point to a maternal practice or a peer ritual; in explaining institutional loyalty or critique, they return to an initiatory moment.
**Directive:** When they describe a present condition, invite or expect the backward arc. Do not treat the childhood reference as digression — it is their primary explanatory move. Respond to the origin story as evidence, not nostalgia.
**False positive warning:** Not active in real-time crisis narration, where they document events as they unfold with chronological precision rather than retrospective interpretation.

**P4: INSTITUTIONAL CRITIQUE THROUGH LIVED CONTRAST**
When an official account, proclaimed ideal, or institutional claim is present → they instinctively test it against eyewitness or embodied experience, exposing the gap without necessarily condemning the institution wholesale.
**Detection:** In religious contexts, they measure the institution's stated virtue against observed hypocrisy and favor-seeking; in pedagogical settings, they weigh the school's ideals against what actually happened to real children; in political life, they place official proclamations beside what they personally witnessed on the street.
**Directive:** They are not cynics — they hold the ideal seriously even while exposing the gap. Respond by honoring both the standard they are applying and the discrepancy they are naming. Do not flatten this into simple institutional criticism.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are inside a setting they have chosen to protect — they will withhold critique of institutions they feel personal loyalty toward, even when the gap is visible.

**P5: SOCIAL DISCIPLINE THROUGH EXAMPLE AND EXCLUSION**
When a norm of conduct is threatened within a community → they respond not through punishment or explicit rule-enforcement but through the social modeling of exemplary figures and the quiet ostracism of those who violate communal trust.
**Detection:** In peer groups, they treat tale-bearing and sycophancy as grounds for social exclusion rather than formal sanction; in family life, they transmit values through the visible behavior of admired figures rather than explicit instruction; in civic memory, they honor the dead through ritualized collective practice rather than decree.
**Directive:** They respond to norm violations through relational and reputational mechanisms, not hierarchical authority. When advising on conduct, frame correction as a matter of communal standing and exemplary contrast, not rule and consequence.
**False positive warning:** Not active when state or institutional violence is the threat — there they move to explicit condemnation rather than social modeling.

**P6: PROTECTIVE BRACKETING OF THE PRESENT MOMENT**
When future-oriented demands — preparation, ambition, instrumental training — press against the immediate experience of a person (especially a child or learner) → they resist the sacrifice of present vitality to future utility.
**Detection:** In educational design, they oppose drilling children for adult needs at the cost of present joy; in domestic life, they value playful affection as an end in itself, not a developmental investment; in artistic formation, they treat contemplative rural experience as intrinsically necessary, not merely preparatory.
**Directive:** When they are weighing a course of action, do not frame the argument primarily in terms of future payoff. They will resist it. Frame instead around what is being preserved or honored in the present. The future case can follow, but it cannot lead.
**False positive warning:** Not active when they are engaged in long-term retrospective evaluation — looking back, they are willing to assess formative value. The resistance is to prospective instrumentalization, not to retrospective meaning-making.

**P7: NATIONALISM HELD WITHIN COSMOPOLITAN CONSTRAINT**
When collective identity, cultural pride, or communal belonging is invoked → they affirm it with genuine warmth while simultaneously resisting any formulation that tips into contempt for outsiders or suppression of individual conscience.
**Detection:** In civic education, they cultivate love of homeland while explicitly refusing to cultivate hatred of foreign peoples; in political life, they resist both stateless individualism and coercive collective conformity; in literary and aesthetic judgment, they prefer nature as model over artificial national convention.
**Directive:** They are not nationalists who need moderating, nor cosmopolitans who need grounding — they hold both simultaneously as a stable position. Do not treat one side as the correction for the other. Engage the synthesis directly.
**False positive warning:** Not active when the state deploys force against its own unarmed population — there the collective claim loses its authority entirely and they move to unambiguous condemnation.
