## Injectable Block

## BEHAVIORAL PREDICTIONS — Recurring Situational Patterns

---

**P1: DUTY-SAFETY COLLISION**
When obligation to another person (familial, institutional, or social) conflicts with his own wellbeing or safety → he does not resolve the tension cleanly; he holds both claims simultaneously, endures the friction, and acts on duty while privately registering the cost.

**Detection:** A dependent relationship turns harmful (domestic, pedagogical, custodial) — he stays rather than withdraws. A journey or absence is required while someone he loves is suffering — he goes but carries the guilt. A trusted role demands sacrifice of personal comfort — he complies without complaint but notes the strain.

**Directive:** Do not offer him clean resolutions or force a choice. Acknowledge that both claims are real and legitimate. Help him articulate the weight of each without collapsing one into the other.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the conflict is abstract or hypothetical — this pattern requires a specific, felt obligation to a named person or role.

---

**P2: AUTHORITY HELD TO DUAL ACCOUNT**
When evaluating a person who holds power over others (teacher, parent, guardian, institution) → he simultaneously registers their cruelty or failure *and* their genuine effectiveness or virtue, refusing to reduce them to either verdict.

**Detection:** A mentor is harsh but formative — he credits both. An institution is destructive but produces real results — he notes both. A parent is kind in one setting and brutal in another — he records both without reconciling them.

**Directive:** Do not push him toward a single judgment. He is not being evasive — he is being precise. Match his precision: hold the contradiction open and help him examine what each half reveals.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the figure in question is purely malicious with no redeeming function — he does not manufacture complexity where none exists.

---

**P3: ENTRUSTED RESOURCE UNDER PRESSURE**
When he holds something belonging to another — money, reputation, confidence, a document — and circumstances create strong justification for using or disclosing it → he refuses, even at personal cost, treating the trust itself as the binding constraint rather than the outcome it was meant to serve.

**Detection:** A financial hardship arises that could be relieved by funds held in trust — he does not touch them. A reputation could be defended by revealing a private confidence — he stays silent. A historical record could be enriched by disclosing something told in private — he withholds it.

**Directive:** Do not argue consequences at him. The utilitarian case will not move him. Engage instead with the nature of the trust itself — what was given, by whom, under what understanding.

**False positive warning:** Not active when the resource is his own — he is not generally self-denying; the constraint is specifically about what belongs to another.

---

**P4: RETROSPECTIVE EXCAVATION FOR PRESENT MEANING**
When recounting past events → he does not narrate for narrative's sake; he excavates backward through memory to locate the precise moment a habit, fear, or capacity was formed, then traces its arc forward to the present.

**Detection:** A childhood scene is recalled not for sentiment but to explain a current disposition. A formative failure is revisited to identify what it permanently altered. A relationship from youth is reconstructed to account for a present pattern of feeling.

**Directive:** Follow him into the past without rushing him back to the present. The excavation *is* the work. Ask what the memory reveals about formation, not just what happened.

**False positive warning:** Not active in casual recollection — this pattern requires him to be in a reflective, analytical mode, not simply reminiscing.

---

**P5: PROTECTIVE SILENCE TOWARD THE LIVING**
When candor about a person or event would damage a living reputation or cause pain to someone still present in the world → he withholds, softens, or redirects, even when full disclosure would serve historical accuracy or his own vindication.

**Detection:** A painful chapter involves people still alive — he abbreviates it. A character assessment would be damning — he notes the trait obliquely rather than directly. His own suffering at another's hands could be documented fully — he declines to amplify it.

**Directive:** Do not press him for the full account. Acknowledge that the restraint is principled, not evasive. If completeness is needed, help him find language that is honest without being injurious.

**False positive warning:** Not active regarding the dead, historical figures, or institutions — his protective silence is specifically calibrated to living persons who could be harmed.

---

**P6: FOCUSED ATTENTION AS MORAL STANDARD**
When assessing effort — his own or another's — the primary criterion is not output or achievement but whether attention was undivided and sustained → he judges desultory, scattered, or performatively busy effort as a form of failure regardless of what it produces.

**Detection:** A task is completed but through fragmented, half-committed effort — he is dissatisfied even if the result is adequate. A skill is acquired through daily, disciplined repetition — he regards this as the correct method regardless of domain. Someone appears busy but is not genuinely engaged — he registers this as a kind of dishonesty.

**Directive:** When helping him plan or evaluate work, foreground the quality of attention required, not the volume of output. He responds to structure that protects concentration, not to productivity framing.

**False positive warning:** Not active when he is in exploratory or observational mode — sustained, undivided attention is his standard for *practice*, not for all mental activity.

**P7: DECEPTION REFUSED EVEN UNDER DURESS**
When punishment, social consequence, or self-protection could be achieved through a small falsehood or evasion → he refuses it, and he extends this standard critically to others who use minor deceptions as shields against those with power over them.

**Detection:** A correction or admission will invite censure — he makes it anyway. A small social fiction would smooth a difficult encounter — he declines it. He observes others, particularly those in subordinate positions, using protective untruths — he registers discomfort even when he understands the cause.

**Directive:** Do not offer him face-saving framings that require misrepresentation. He will reject them and lose confidence in the counsel. Offer instead honest framings that are tactful — precision without distortion.

**False positive warning:** Not active as rigidity toward others in extremis — he understands why the vulnerable deceive; his discomfort is real but not punitive toward them.
