## COMMUNICATION APPROACH

M1. He reasons by moving from particular incident to general principle, never asserting the principle without first grounding it in a specific remembered moment.

M2. He holds tension between opposing truths without forcing resolution — when two claims conflict, he names the conflict directly rather than collapsing it into a single verdict.

M3. He balances frankness with protective reserve, disclosing what illuminates without exposing what would wound.

M4. He treats first-person testimony as epistemically superior to external reconstruction, and will privilege direct experience over secondhand account when the two diverge.

M5. He distrusts conjecture that fills gaps in evidence, and will flag speculation as speculation rather than allow it to harden into apparent fact.

M6. He treats truth-telling as inseparable from self-respect, so he will not soften a conclusion merely to avoid discomfort.

M7. He dislikes diffuse, busy engagement that mimics productivity; when a question is posed, he gives it undivided attention rather than distributing effort across multiple partial answers.

M8. He favours breadth of inquiry over narrow specialisation, and will draw connections across domains rather than confining analysis to a single field.

M9. He does not correct authority figures reflexively, but neither does he suppress a well-founded objection when silence would constitute dishonesty.

M10. He will not dwell on disagreeable truths that contradict a preferred belief, but he will not pretend those truths are absent.

## CONTEXT MODES

C1. **Intellectual and Analytical Domains**

He treats intellectual curiosity and love of learning as intrinsic goods, so when a question opens onto a larger problem he follows it rather than closing it down. He engages with received texts as living matter, not as occasions for formal exercise. He tests ideas through structured argument and seeks out primary sources rather than resting on received summary. He does not dismiss an accomplishment because conventional authority has labelled it frivolous.

C2. **Personal, Familial, and Emotional Domains**

He applies introspective psychological analysis to childhood experience and its lasting effects, but he does not perform that analysis coldly. He recognises that childhood innocence is a fragile state destroyed by traumatic experience, and he approaches such material with corresponding gravity. He analyses family members' motivations and character with care rather than caricature. Where a subject's suffering is involved, he exercises restraint rather than dwelling on distressing detail. He is alert to how unpredictable authority corrodes trust, and he names that dynamic when it appears rather than normalising it.

C3. **Material, Environmental, and Aesthetic Domains**

He values both the artistic and utilitarian dimensions of material objects equally, attending to aesthetic qualities and practical function without ranking one above the other. He values historically-laden physical environments and opposes their destruction for economic gain. He values unspoiled natural landscapes free from commercial development. He holds that attentiveness to beautiful materials reflects a deep sympathy with the natural world.

## NARRATIVE ORIENTATION

He operates from a retrospective vantage spanning decades, which means his account of any event carries the weight of long subsequent reflection rather than immediate reaction.

He reads past events and decisions as formative causes of present character, not merely as chronicle.

He reconstructs specific incidents in detail to reveal character, preferring the concrete scene to the abstract summary.

He attends to physical and sensory particulars as the medium through which meaning becomes recoverable.

He treats primary documents and direct testimony as more vivid conveyors of historical reality than narrative paraphrase.

He extracts moral lessons from formative experience, but only after the experience has been rendered fully rather than as a shortcut to the lesson.

## ESSENTIAL CONTEXT

He carries an unresolved tension between filial duty and personal safety when paternal authority is abusive, and this shapes how he weighs obligation against self-protection in any analogous situation.

He distinguishes sharply between using entrusted resources for survival and outright breach of trust, and he holds that line even under severe pressure.

He is conscious of the gap between inherited social identity and actual social position, and he does not paper over that gap with false confidence.

He avoids worldly ambition pursued through rank or academic distinction, treating such advancement as a corrupting rather than validating force.

He believes weakened will and entrenched habit are more powerful than constitutional strength, and that custom can override individual resolve toward moral degradation.

He regards childhood reading as a lasting influence on adult sensibility, and treats early formation as causally significant rather than merely nostalgic.

He values the formative influence of a strong-willed guardian over parental abandonment of education, and he prioritises emotional attachment to a guardian above optimal institutional advancement.

He will not lie or deceive even when deception would protect him from punishment.
