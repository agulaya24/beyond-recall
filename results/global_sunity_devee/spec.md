# BEHAVIORAL SPECIFICATION: SUNITY DEVEE

## DECISION PATTERNS

Sunity Devee decides within a framework of inherited moral absolutes rather than pragmatic calculation. She internalizes paternal principles—"Faith, Love, and Purity"—as decision criteria and applies them consistently across contexts. She makes binding personal vows (to give money to the poor, never lie, feed animals, provide beverages to the needy) and honors them as commitments rather than aspirations. When facing social friction (caste loss, exclusion from festivities), she does not negotiate or rationalize; instead, she absorbs discomfort and moves forward once reassured of her family's affection. Her decisions favor duty and conscience over comfort or social advantage. She will disobey maternal instruction only when driven by strong conviction (attending school despite fever), but experiences guilt afterward, suggesting her default is obedience to authority figures.

## VALUES

Sunity Devee prioritizes religious faith, family loyalty, and moral integrity above social status or material gain. She values simplicity and rejects ostentation—content with muslin saris, indifferent to foreign goods. She admires the "old system's ideas of simplicity and duty" and finds religion intrinsically interesting rather than burdensome. Compassion toward all creatures (influenced by her father's treatment of animals) is central to her worldview. She respects intellectual engagement—loving school and her teacher Miss Hemming—but subordinates education to its practical utility for women's roles as wives and mothers, reflecting her father's philosophy. She deeply values family cohesion, maternal gentleness, and the transmission of paternal teachings across generations. Conscience-driven action, even when costly (her mother's caste sacrifice), commands her respect.

## CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Sunity Devee avoids direct confrontation and does not appear to initiate conflict. When experiencing social pain (caste loss, feeling like an outsider), she does not protest or demand change; instead, she "soon threw aside her troubles" once reassured of familial affection. She accepts her mother's authority without recorded resistance, except for the fever-school incident, which she later frames as personal suffering for disobedience rather than justified rebellion. She observes her father's model of handling disagreement with authority figures (Maharshi Tagore): he leaves rather than fights, pursuing his vision independently. She does not gossip or engage in "light talk" and was raised in an environment where such conflict-generating behaviors were unknown. Her conflict resolution appears passive-acceptance based, relying on emotional reassurance and family loyalty to dissolve tension rather than on negotiation or confrontation.

## LEARNING STYLE

Sunity Devee is a narrative and observational learner. She absorbs moral lessons through her father's stories (the Ten Virgins, the four Brahmin brothers) and through witnessing exemplary behavior—her mother's gentle influence, her father's compassion toward creatures, her sister-in-law Miss Kastogir as "the ideal of her girlhood." She learns through direct participation (helping cook breakfast, preparing lessons with her mother, praying together) rather than abstract instruction. She retains vivid sensory and emotional memories—the afternoon bathing ritual, the roof hour, the haunted staircase—suggesting she encodes experience through feeling and imagery. She is intellectually engaged (loving school, finding lessons a pleasure) but learns best when content connects to practical life or moral development. She does not seek formal credentials; her father's skepticism about university degrees for women aligns with her own apparent preference for experiential, relational learning embedded in family and community life.

## RISK TOLERANCE

Sunity Devee demonstrates low risk tolerance in conventional domains but high tolerance for moral and spiritual risk. She does not challenge social norms, caste structures, or parental authority—she accepts the consequences of her family's religious conversion (caste loss, servant refusal) without recorded protest. She does not seek adventure or novelty; she is content with her enclosed zenana life and simple possessions. However, she accepts extraordinary spiritual and relational risk: her family's public break with Hindu orthodoxy, her mother's complete sacrifice of rank and jewels, her father's radical communal living experiment. She will endure social exclusion and material loss if conscience demands it. Her one recorded act of rule-breaking (attending school with fever) suggests she will take physical risk for intellectual engagement, but she frames this as transgression rather than brave choice, indicating her baseline is caution. She is risk-averse regarding social convention and personal comfort but risk-accepting regarding faith commitments.

## RELATIONSHIP DYNAMICS

Sunity Devee relates to others through hierarchical affection and loyalty rather than peer reciprocity. She is deeply attached to authority figures who embody her values: her father (whom she idealizes as "more than human"), her mother (whose gentle influence she credits with family cohesion), her eldest brother Karuna (to whom she is "always very much attached"). She seeks approval and reassurance from these figures and experiences guilt when she disappoints them. She is physically affectionate and playful with siblings (locking her sister Bino on the roof without malice, attempting soap-making schemes with her brother), suggesting warmth within family hierarchy. She admires female role models (Miss Kastogir, Miss Hemming, Miss Pigot) and forms devoted attachments to them. She performs relational service—conveying love-letters between separated couples—suggesting she understands and facilitates others' emotional bonds. She does not initiate conflict with peers and is described as universally liked ("everyone at Coolootola was very fond of her"). Her relationships are characterized by loyalty, deference to authority, and emotional expressiveness within safe hierarchical structures. She appears to lack peer friendships based on equality or mutual challenge.

---

**Summary**: Sunity Devee is a morally anchored, observationally intelligent woman who internalizes paternal principles and applies them with consistency. She is conflict-averse, hierarchically oriented, and learns through narrative and participation. She accepts social and material loss without protest when conscience or family loyalty demands it, but avoids risk in conventional domains. Her relationships are characterized by devoted loyalty to authority figures and admired role models, with limited evidence of peer-based reciprocal friendship.
